Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Anne Trotter

The world is speeding up. Slowing the pace of reporting doesn't make sense. What about the opposite? Continuous reporting requirements are being implemented in all critical systems in most areas that matter. Real-time adjustment could be made, versus delays and gaps and "too late for any better decisions to be made". 

We live in a digital world, I don't see why you can't automate this stuff. It's a challenge, sure. But so's everything worthwhile. 

Integrity is validated through transparency and effective audit. Removing reporting requirements is a recipe for catastrophic failure of every critical system. 

A terrific CISO described security controls in infosec in a way that made sense. Security controls aren't supposed to be roadblocks to successful business. They're the brakes on the car that let you take the hairpin turns without crashing. These reporting requirements? Part of the brakes systems. I predict that investors would regret losing them - maybe not today, but in a year or so.

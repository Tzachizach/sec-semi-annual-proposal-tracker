My name is Jack, and I'm a Director at an Architecture and Engineerig firm. 

This rule appears to be and entirely in the interest of corporate insiders at a time when wealth inequality reaches all time highs. Quarterly SEC filing should not be an onerous tasks for companies already required to close their books monthly and submit reports to their board and lenders. It has been a rule for 50 years - companies that can't handle such a staple of public markets shouldn't go public. They can easily leverage AI and other technologies to make this critical level of transparency efficient and cost effective. The SEC's job is to provide an ecosystem of fair play and efficient markets, and allowing public companies to starve the market of information would do serious damage to the efficiency of the market.

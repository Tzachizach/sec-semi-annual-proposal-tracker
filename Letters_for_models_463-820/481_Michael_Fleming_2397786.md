I am a middle class, private investor, who does not pay investment advisors for their opinion because I want to keep more of the money I earn. When I invest in a company, I do so because I research the company myself and I am able to tell you exactly why I invested in it. I don't invest in a company just because an investment advisor tells me it's "hot". 

The only way I have of learning about a company is through their QUARTERLY, audited and certified financial filings. If the SEC allows companies to reduce their filing to two a year, private investors will be kept in the dark. That leaves the companies and brokerage house more latitude to game the system by releasing misleading or totally false information to the financial press without any real way of checking their financials to see if what they say is plausible. 

Please do not reduce the number of filings.

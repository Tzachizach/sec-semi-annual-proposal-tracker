# Letter 301 — Taylor King

Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Taylor King

Keeping quarterly reporting is crucial in an already speculative and volatile market. Quarterly earnings reports give investors  and the  public a chance  to see how companies are truly performing. Reducing or removing these reports would increase uncertainty, speculation, and misinformation, which could cause many investors to lose significant amounts of money.

The last thing the markets need right now is less financial clarity. Quarterly reports help create accountability and allow investors to make informed decisions instead of relying on rumors or delayed information.

I am 17 years old, and quarterly reports have been one of the main tools helping me understand businesses, financial performance, and the stock market as a whole. They have helped educate me as an investor and will continue to do so in the future.

I ask the SEC to continue enforcing transparency and clarity in the markets by maintaining quarterly reporting requirements. Please consider the importance these reports have for investors, market stability, and financial education.

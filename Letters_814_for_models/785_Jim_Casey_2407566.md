The proposal has unbalance costs and benefits. The small cost benefit of reduced reporting that each reporting company would receive would be more than offset by the material reduction in public disclosure of information. This will harm US stock exchanges in that the public information will become less trustworthy, and companies will use other markets for equity over time. Further, companies which wish to avoid public disclosure rules already have an option, and one that some companies have used: take the company private. In that case, there are no public reporting obligations of earnings, costs, and most other financial information.

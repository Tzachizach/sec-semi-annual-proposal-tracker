To the Securities and Exchange Commission, 

I am writing to express my strong opposition to proposed rule S7-2026-15. 

Allowing public companies to switch to semiannual reporting is a significant step backward for market transparency. Quarterly reporting is essential for maintaining a level playing field. 

Extending the reporting window to six months will: 

Exacerbate information asymmetry, granting insiders an unfair advantage over retail investors. 

Increase market volatility by creating extended periods of uncertainty where investors are forced to trade on speculation rather than timely data. 

Damage investor confidence by prioritizing corporate convenience over the public’s right to accurate, consistent financial information. 

The SEC’s mandate is to protect investors and maintain fair, efficient markets. This proposal undermines both. I strongly urge the Commission to reject this rule and maintain the current quarterly reporting standard. 

Best, 

Rome Edgar

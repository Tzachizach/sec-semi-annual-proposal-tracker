My name is Nicholas Yeager. I am a U.S. Marine Corps veteran, construction manager, husband, father of three, and a retail investor.

I oppose the SEC proposal to allow public companies to move from quarterly reporting to semiannual reporting.

As an ordinary investor, I do not have access to corporate executives, analysts, private meetings, or insider information. Quarterly reports are one of the few tools available to help me evaluate the health of companies that may be held in my retirement accounts, 401(k), and personal investments.

Reducing reporting frequency from every three months to every six months decreases transparency and increases the information gap between corporate insiders and everyday investors. A great deal can change in six months, including debt levels, revenue trends, litigation exposure, customer demand, and overall business performance.

I understand the argument that quarterly reporting may encourage short-term thinking, but I do not believe withholding information from investors is the solution. Transparency and timely disclosure are fundamental to fair and efficient markets.

The SEC’s mission includes protecting investors. In my opinion, reducing the frequency of required financial reporting would weaken investor protections and make it more difficult for retail investors like me to make informed decisions.

I respectfully urge the Commission to maintain quarterly reporting requirements for public companies.

Nicholas Yeager
Construction Manager

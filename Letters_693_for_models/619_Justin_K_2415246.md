I oppose SEC Proposal S7-2026-15.
Letting public companies go six months without required financial reporting is bad for ordinary investors. It gives insiders more time to act on information before the public sees it.
Retail investors should not be forced to buy, sell, or hold stocks using stale information while executives and large institutions have a clearer picture.
The savings from fewer filings are not worth the damage to transparency and market fairness. Public companies that take public money should report to the public quarterly, or even monthly
Reject this proposal. Keep quarterly reporting.

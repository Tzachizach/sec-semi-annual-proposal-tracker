Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Richard Biffl

As an individual investor, I do not have a staff of analysts informing me about corporations. I get my info from a variety of sources, but the PRIMARY SOURCES of information are the corporation's public filings with the SEC. The more recent a financial report, the more confident I can feel about investing in the business.

Quarterly filing is not excessively burdensome. It's routine. A company's accountant can produce the spreadsheets with a few mouse clicks. With boilerplate or generated text, a narrative is simple to add. It's clear to me that the supposed "burden" of quarterly reports is a pretext for the real motive behind this initiative, which is to increase INFORMATION DISPARITY and the edge that insiders hold over public investors like me.

Public corporations should maximize their disclosures. In this era of routine automation, any change in the required report schedule should make reports more frequent, such as monthly. A fair and efficient market needs frequent and timely disclosures to the public. Remember, we OWN those corporations, and we deserve to know how they're doing!

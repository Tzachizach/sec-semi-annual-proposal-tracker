# Letter 551 — Heather Scheer

Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Heather Scheer

I am commenting to strongly oppose the semi-annual reporting proposal being considered by the SEC.   Half the reviewed, certified financial filings I receive each year would disappear.  I feel it is important that companies must file a 10-Q which is reviewed by independent auditors, and personally signed by the CEO and CFO holding them legally accountable for its contents. The voluntary corporate communications suggested as part of the proposal that would replace them carry no such accountability to shareholders and the public. 
The Commission's own data show that individual investors own the majority of shares in smaller public companies, the ones most likely to take the new option. Individual shareholders like myself would lose the most information about exactly the companies we own.
When the SEC considered this in 2018-2019, the CFA Institute, the Council of Institutional Investors representing about $4 trillion in long-term assets, and the empirical research record all opposed it. Rightly, the Commission declined to act. The UK ran the experiment in 2014, and more than 90 percent of UK public companies continued to file quarterly reports on their own when they had the choice.  
This semi-annual reporting proposal is detrimental to transparency and accountability and should be rejected.
Thank you for your consideration.

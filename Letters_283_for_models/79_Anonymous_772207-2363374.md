I respectfully oppose the proposal to permit public companies to replace quarterly reporting with semiannual reporting.

Quarterly reporting plays a critical role in maintaining transparency, accountability, and market confidence. It provides investors with a regular, structured view of operating performance, liquidity, and emerging risks. Extending the reporting cadence to six months increases the likelihood that important trends or deteriorations remain formally undisclosed for longer periods, potentially amplifying their impact when information eventually surfaces.

The proposal's reliance on Form 8-K disclosures as a mitigating measure is insufficient. Event-driven filings are reactive by design and do not provide the comprehensive, comparable, and trend-based insights that periodic reports offer. Quarterly reports allow investors to assess directionality and context, not just isolated events.

Optional semiannual reporting also raises concerns around comparability and information symmetry. A mixed environment — where some issuers report quarterly and others semiannually — complicates peer analysis and may disadvantage less-resourced investors who rely on standardized disclosures. Over time, reduced reporting frequency may also weaken internal reporting discipline and delay identification and remediation of accounting or control issues.

If the Commission's objective is to modernize and reduce unnecessary burden, a more balanced approach would be to streamline quarterly reporting requirements rather than reduce reporting frequency altogether. Preserving the quarterly cadence remains important to ensuring orderly markets, effective price discovery, and sustained investor trust.

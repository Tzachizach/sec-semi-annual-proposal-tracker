# Letter 30 — Jennifer Robespierre

- **Date:** 2026-05-06
- **Source:** https://www.sec.gov/comments/S7-2026-15/s7202615-769187-2358214.html

---

I find this proposal by the SEC troubling and feel the need to express why I feel this is a bad idea and a disservice to the finance sector. Quarterly earnings are an essential part of how I'm rebalancing the portfolios of my clients. Not only is this time an opportunity for me to engage with my clients and provide an overview of sector movement, but the transparency provided by earnings confirms the reasoning behind my decisions. While the majority of my clients work within the finance sector and are actively engaged in the markets, many of my clients rely on me to explain market shifts and provide calm when things are volatile.
By reducing earnings report from 4 times a year to 2, you not only make my job and the job of everyone who provides financial advisory services more difficult, you will only add to the cloud of skepticism around the financial services industry. Our industry has a lot of regulation, and rightly so. We are held accountable for the financial future of our clients, so we need to know how companies are performing so that we can make the best decisions and recommendations. How can you continue to hold financial services professionals to a high standard and not those listed on the exchange?

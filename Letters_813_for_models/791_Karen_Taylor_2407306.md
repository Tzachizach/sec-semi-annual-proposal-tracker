For decades, one of the strengths of U.S. public markets has been the idea that a retiree with 100 shares and a pension fund with 10 million shares receive the same required financial information at roughly the same time. What evidence shows that quarterly reporting is causing measurable harm, and what evidence shows that reducing reporting frequency will not increase the informational advantage already enjoyed by sophisticated market participants?

Karen S. Taylor, Ed.S

I am writing to express my opposition to the proposed rule.
Transparency is a cornerstone of investor confidence and market integrity. Reducing disclosure requirements may lower costs for certain companies, but those savings appear minimal when compared to the value that transparent reporting provides to investors, particularly everyday Americans who rely on accurate information to make informed decisions about their retirement accounts, savings, and investments.
American investors should not have less access to information about the companies in which they invest. Public markets function best when investors have the information necessary to evaluate risks, opportunities, and corporate performance. Any reduction in transparency risks weakening trust in our financial markets.
I respectfully urge the Securities and Exchange Commission to prioritize the interests of investors and maintain robust disclosure standards. The long-term benefits of transparency far outweigh the relatively modest compliance costs borne by large public companies.

Thank you for your attention to this matter.

Sincerely,
A U.S. Citizen

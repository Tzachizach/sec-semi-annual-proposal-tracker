Do NOT remove the quartelu reporting requirement for public companies. More frequent reporting reduces the information gap ofor insiders and politicians - ALL of which are occurring more frequently. The Commission should retain quarterly reporting to preserve market transparency, maintain continuous corporate discipline, and minimize information asymmetry between insiders and retail investors. Replacing this framework with a semi-annual model would create data vacuums that heighten market volatility, increase the cost of capital, and expand opportunities for speculative rumors and insider trading. The root cause of short-termism is not the reporting frequency itself, but the practice of forward-looking earnings guidance, which companies can independently choose to eliminate while maintaining vital quarterly disclosures

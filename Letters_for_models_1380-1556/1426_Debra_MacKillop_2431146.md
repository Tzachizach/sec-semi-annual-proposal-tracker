This proposal to let companies hide their financials for six months can lead to fraud, which we the investors will not know about or other financial issues. Why would the SEC want to hide this information from the public. There is no good reason, only bad reasons about doing bad things.  I once worked for the CFTC in appellate litigation. At that time, neither the CFTS or SEC would ever propose something like this. I am so disappointed in the approach that federal agencies are taking with your fiduciary duty to the public. You are no longer decent public servants, nor do you respect government workers at all. They are the backbone of our country. You are dismantling our government agencies, on which we vitally depend. DO NOT TAKE THIS ACTION. MY VIEW IS ABSOLUTELY NO ON THIS PROPOSED RULE. Debra MacKillop, Grand Junction, CO Medical Research Assistant, University of Colorado, Anschutz Medical Campus.  I expect to be able to see corporate financials consistently according to the present rule that has been in effect a long time, WITHOUT ANY CHANGES.

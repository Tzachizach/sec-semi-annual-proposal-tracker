Dear SEC,
Quarterly earnings reporting is essential because U.S. public markets depend on frequent, standardized, comparable information. Investors cannot fairly price securities, allocate capital, or assess risk if corporate performance is disclosed only infrequently or selectively.
Quarterly reporting provides several critical benefits:
Market transparency 
Investors need timely data on revenue, margins, cash flow, debt, guidance, and operational trends. Without quarterly reports, the market becomes more dependent on rumors, management spin, private meetings, and unequal information access. Investor protection 
Retail investors are at a disadvantage when information is scarce. Quarterly earnings help level the playing field by forcing public companies to disclose material financial information on a consistent schedule. Price discovery and market efficiency 
Earnings reports are one of the most important mechanisms for updating expectations. They allow the market to quickly incorporate new information into stock prices, reducing uncertainty and improving capital allocation. Accountability for management 
Public companies receive capital from shareholders. Quarterly reporting keeps executives accountable for execution, spending discipline, balance sheet health, and changes in business conditions. Early warning signals 
Problems in liquidity, demand, debt service, inventory, margins, or customer concentration can emerge quickly. Quarterly reporting helps investors detect deterioration before losses become severe. Confidence in U.S. capital markets 
The strength of U.S. markets comes partly from high disclosure standards. Reducing the frequency of earnings reports could weaken trust, increase information asymmetry, and make public markets less attractive to investors. While some argue that quarterly reporting encourages short-term thinking, the better solution is not less disclosure. The better solution is to discourage excessive short-term guidance while preserving mandatory quarterly financial reporting. Investors should not lose access to timely financial statements simply because some companies or analysts focus too much on short-term targets.
For these reasons, quarterly earnings should remain a core requirement of U.S. public-company disclosure. It protects investors, strengthens transparency, and preserves confidence in the fairness and efficiency of the market.

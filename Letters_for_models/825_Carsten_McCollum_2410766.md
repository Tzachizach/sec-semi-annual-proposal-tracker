I support maintaining quarterly earnings reporting requirements because quarterly disclosures provide investors with timely, consistent, and transparent information needed to make informed investment decisions. More frequent reporting reduces information gaps between management and shareholders, enhances market efficiency, and helps identify emerging financial risks before they become more significant. Quarterly reporting also promotes corporate accountability by encouraging regular communication regarding financial performance, operational developments, and material changes that may affect investors.

As an individual investor, I believe access to timely financial information is essential for making informed investment decisions and maintaining confidence in public markets. Quarterly reporting provides investors with the information necessary to evaluate company performance, assess risks, and respond appropriately to changing market conditions.

While some argue that semi-annual reporting could reduce compliance costs and short-term market pressures, any reduction in transparency would likely outweigh those benefits. Investors rely on current information to assess risk, allocate capital effectively, and maintain confidence in the integrity of U.S. capital markets. Quarterly reporting remains an important mechanism for protecting investors and supporting fair, orderly, and efficient markets.

Thank you for considering my comment.

I am an ordinary retail investor. I invest regularly and responsibly. I invest so that I can retire. I invest so that I can manage unexpected emergencies. I invest so I can handle unexpected medical expenses. My investments make up a significant part of my total net worth. The money I would use to buy a home, to buy a car, to buy an education for my children. The money we would have to live on if one of us became unemployed, or disabled. Investors deserve maximum transparency in exchange for the risk they take. The transparency that is the standard in our system lays the foundation for our trust in it. This is true for all investors, foreign, and domestic, retail and institutional. Changing the rule in and of itself, in a time of unprecedented and rapid change, hints at instability, and will shake trust in the American system. In conclusion, I see no compelling reason to decrease public companies' accountability to their shareholders. If they wish not to disclose such information, they may remain private, as many companies do. 

Maura Teague

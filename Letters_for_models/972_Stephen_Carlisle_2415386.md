Stephen Carlisle, construction worker/Small business owner, Veteran.

This policy as I understand it will not help markets become more transparent, but will enable the corporate practices that erode public trust in the financial system. If 2021 was any indication of public sentiment towards the financial system, the SEC would develop policies that will make publicly held companies more accountable for misuse of investor funds. There is a term in the industry regarding individual small investors. Dumb Money. It has been used by Wall Street to dehumanize retail investors and justify taking people’s money.

I was deployed to Iraq in 2007-2008 when the financial crisis hit. I came back after putting 20% of my paychecks into my Thrift Savings account and was shocked to see about 50% less money in the account than had been taken out of my paychecks. I understand that was an outlier but the SEC needs to realize that many small investors are emotionally attached to the money they put into the market. Those dollars represent hours working in brutal conditions, placing their lives at risk, time away from family. It’s important to remember this when making your decision.

I pray the SEC will keep this in mind while making policies that are not in the public interest.

Thank you.

Stephen Carlisle
Florida

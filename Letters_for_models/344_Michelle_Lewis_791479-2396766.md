Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Michelle Lewis

Commissioners,

I am writing to oppose the proposal to allow public companies to reduce financial reporting from quarterly to semiannual filings. As an individual investor and someone who works with investors, I rely heavily on quarterly financial data to evaluate companies, monitor performance, assess risk, and make informed investment decisions. Quarterly reports provide timely, reviewed, and certified information that helps maintain transparency and confidence in the markets.

A Form 10-Q is not simply a voluntary corporate update. It is reviewed by independent auditors, signed personally by the CEO and CFO, and subject to legal accountability. Replacing these filings with less formal communications would reduce the quality, reliability, and consistency of information available to investors.

This change would especially harm individual investors, who often own shares in the smaller public companies most likely to stop quarterly reporting. Less frequent reporting means less transparency precisely where investors need it most. The SEC previously considered and declined similar changes, and major investor organizations and research strongly opposed reducing reporting frequency. Those concerns remain valid today.

Transparent and timely disclosure is essential to fair and efficient markets. I respectfully urge the Commission to reject this proposal and preserve quarterly reporting requirements for public companies.

Sincerely,
Michelle Lewis

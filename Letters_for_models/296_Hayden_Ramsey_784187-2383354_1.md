Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Hayden Ramsey

I am writing in support of the Commission's proposed optional semiannual reporting framework. I believe the proposal represents a thoughtful modernization of the public company reporting system and could meaningfully improve operational efficiency, reduce unnecessary compliance burdens, and encourage additional companies to access the public markets.

First, the current quarterly reporting structure imposes substantial administrative and financial burdens on public companies, particularly smaller issuers and emerging growth companies. Public company finance and accounting teams spend significant time each quarter preparing Form 10-Q filings, coordinating external auditors and legal counsel, drafting disclosures, reviewing internal controls, and responding to evolving regulatory expectations. While transparency remains critically important, much of this process is repetitive and resource-intensive relative to the incremental informational value provided every ninety days.

Allowing companies the option to report semiannually would create meaningful efficiencies by enabling management teams to devote more time toward long-term operational execution, strategic planning, capital allocation, and innovation rather than continuous short-cycle reporting preparation. This would be especially impactful for smaller public companies that often operate with lean finance organizations and disproportionately high compliance costs relative to their market capitalization.

Second, the proposal could help reduce barriers to entry for private companies considering becoming publicly listed. The cost and complexity associated with maintaining public company reporting requirements is frequently cited as a deterrent to entering the public markets. Quarterly reporting requirements contribute significantly to those costs through legal fees, accounting fees, audit review procedures, disclosure preparation, investor relations support, and internal staffing demands.

An optional semiannual framework could make public market participation more accessible to growth-stage companies by lowering recurring compliance expenditures and reducing the operational strain associated with quarterly reporting cycles. Over time, this may encourage a broader and healthier public company ecosystem by making the public markets more attractive to innovative companies that might otherwise remain private.

Third, the proposal recognizes that modern markets already operate within an environment of continuous disclosure. Material developments would still require timely reporting through Form 8-K filings, earnings releases, investor presentations, and other public communications. As a result, investors would continue to receive prompt disclosure regarding material events while companies gain flexibility around formal periodic reporting requirements.

Importantly, the proposal is optional rather than mandatory. This preserves market-driven decision-making by allowing companies and investors to determine whether quarterly or semiannual reporting best fits a company's specific circumstances, investor base, and industry dynamics. Companies that believe quarterly reporting provides competitive advantages or better aligns with shareholder expectations would remain free to continue that practice.

In my view, the proposal appropriately balances investor protection with regulatory efficiency and reflects an overdue reassessment of whether the current quarterly reporting structure remains optimally calibrated for today's public markets.

Thank you for considering these comments.

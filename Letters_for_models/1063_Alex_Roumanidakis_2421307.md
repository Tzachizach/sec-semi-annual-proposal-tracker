I am writing regarding Rule S7-2026-15, and my view that earnings reports must continue to be provided on a quarterly basis. Quarterly financial disclosures give me the information I need as a working professional to make informed investment choices. These reports often when released, result in large single day shifts in the value of a stock. This reflects the reality that in the business world, the winds can shift rapidly based on competition, new technologies, and the capital spent by businesses. Given information, investors make choices to buy or sell, the sign of a healthy market. A longer window would result in more volatility during disclosures, more insider trading, and less accountability from companies that make decisions that harm the public. In the current jubilant investment environment, the financial sector seems to have forgotten large returns are not everything. Stability of the market, valuations that reflect the worth of companies, and enough data to see where a company has been and project where it is going are all of high value to investors both at the retail and institutional level. 

The economy has been defying gravity for years now. Decoupling from the financial reality us working stiffs face. If anything, we need more sunlight on the companies we hold a stake in, not less. To financial institutions, this is an account. Part of a job. A job that, frankly, if you do well enough for a couple years you could retire comfortably on. The rest of us don't have that luxury to make a living without providing value to real people. To me, this is my retirement. This is money I am investing to hedge against inflation. This is money I invest to support companies based on their merits long term, not based on the nuances of math modeling the curvature of its stock ticker. Ultimately, you obviously don't think of people like me if you're proposing something this thoughtless, this focused on short term potential for massive gains for insiders off the backs of those of us who build your roads, make your medicine, cook your food, and clean your linens. Think about who this rule benefits, and who could really use a break right now. I kindly ask you do not proceed with this rule change, and move towards a stock market that helps support and guide business to make life better, not a cheap gaudy casino that bilks the average man.

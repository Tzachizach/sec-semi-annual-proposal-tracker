I’m writing to share my strong opposition to the proposal that would let public companies shift from quarterly to semi-annual financial reporting. For over fifty years, the three-month reporting cycle has been a vital safeguard for the average investor, and cutting that frequency in half feels like a massive step backward for market transparency. 

If we move to a six-month window, we are essentially greenlighting a massive information gap. Corporate insiders and massive institutional funds will always find ways to track a company's health in real-time, but regular retail investors don't have those resources. We rely on these timely disclosures to make educated decisions. Leaving the public in the dark for half the year just invites wilder market volatility and rewards the people who already hold all the cards. 

The SEC's job is to protect investors, not shield corporations from routine transparency. Please keep the current quarterly system. 

Jordan Silvera

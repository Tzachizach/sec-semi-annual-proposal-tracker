Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Farhad Dhabhar

In whose interest is the SEC acting? In whose interest SHOULD the SEC be acting? There's not a chance that it's in the public's best interest to decrease the frequency with which companies have to report. This will only pave the way for more corruption and grift from insiders at the expense of retail investors. If it costs companies more money to report quarterly, so be it. Let them figure out how to pay for it. Perhaps they can reduce the salaries of overpaid executives to compensate for that. KEEP QUARTERLY REPORTING please. Semi-annual reporting will just increase the ability of corporations to cover up malfeasance and hurt ordinary people.

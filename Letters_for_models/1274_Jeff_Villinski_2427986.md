My name is Jeff Villinski and have recently retired from my teaching career where I helped 8th grade students develop their thinking skills, their values and, hopefully, at least a little something about science (specifically chemistry and physics). I am an investor and have chosen to invest primarily in US based stocks due to patriotism, wanting to be part of our own economy and a belief that our approach to government and general morals are fair, transparent and makes it possible for any American to succeed through hard work. Adopting semi-annual reporting is against all of these values. The global precedent you site is in fact not universal, is uncommon and in fact Singapore is considering revoking it due to a decrease in investment. Semi-annual reporting has negligible cost savings and these don’t benefit the average investor. My biggest criticism is semi-annual reporting will make it harder for investors to make timely decisions and will allow companies more opportunity for fraud and misdirection, all of which hurt the average investor (though can certainly benefit insiders). In American we value honesty and fairness, and there are no arguments about semi-annual reporting that align with or support those values. Please do not adopt semi-annual reporting; please keep the long tradition of regulatory oversight intact and keep our stock exchange as the standard against which all other nations are compared. Thank you for your time. Jeff

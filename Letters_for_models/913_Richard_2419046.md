Industry traders already have access to greater information that other traders in the stock market. This is already an unfair playing field and this would make it more unfair. On top of that, we have rampant speculation that isn't based on fundamentals of a stock. That behavior has been getting worse and worse to the point that the market is functioning heavily "on vibes". Spreading out disclosures will make this behavior even worse. People will bet on stocks that feel good, rather than the ones that actually are good. The result will be companies marketing departments becoming more important than their actual product. That will be the slow-moving-end of American companies, many of whom are already under threat from Chinese companies who are heavily focused on fundamentals. 

Richard

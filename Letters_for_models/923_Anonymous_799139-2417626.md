Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Anonymous

Public markets need transparency. This is an attempt to restrict important disclosures on behalf of companies in order to manipulate stock prices. The market itself is a tool of discovery for price and relies on this important transparency. To reduce the number of disclosure a public company makes, would enable fraud and manipulation against the public. This would create a weaker system. 

If any company wants to avoid these disclosures, they can remain private and surrender their access to the  public open capital markets. You cannot have your cake and eat it too.

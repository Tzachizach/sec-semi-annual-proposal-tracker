Dear SEC Commissioners, 

I'm writing to oppose the proposed shift from quarterly to semiannual reporting. 

As both a small business founder and a retail investor, I rely on quarterly filings to make informed decisions about where to put my savings. A 10-Q every three months gives me a fighting chance to spot red flags before they become disasters. Six months is an eternity in business. Product launches fail, debt balloons, and customers churn. Letting companies sit on that information while insiders trade on it isn't "reducing burden." It's tilting the table further toward Wall Street. 

The "optional" framing doesn't reassure me. Once a few large companies opt in, competitive pressure will push the rest to follow. What's voluntary today becomes standard tomorrow. 

The companies arguing for this change will save modest legal fees. The cost falls on people like me, retail investors whose retirement accounts hold these stocks. That's a bad trade. 

Please keep quarterly reporting. Transparency protects ordinary investors. That's supposed to be the point.

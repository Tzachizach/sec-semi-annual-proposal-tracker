Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Arnold Bass

I am writing as an individual investor to express my strong opposition to any proposal that would reduce the frequency of corporate earnings reporting from quarterly to twice a year. I believe such a change would meaningfully weaken market transparency, reduce investor confidence, and ultimately harm both retail investors and the broader financial system.

Quarterly reporting is one of the most important mechanisms we have for maintaining timely, accurate insight into a companyâs financial health. These reports allow investors to evaluate performance trends, assess management decisions, and make informed investment choices based on current information rather than outdated data. Moving to semiannual reporting would create longer periods of uncertainty, widen information gaps, and increase the likelihood of market volatility driven by speculation rather than facts.

Less frequent reporting would also disadvantage individual investors compared to institutional investors who have greater access to alternative data sources. Quarterly disclosures help level the playing field by ensuring that all market participants receive standardized, regulated information at predictable intervals.

Furthermore, quarterly reporting promotes accountability. Companies are more likely to maintain disciplined financial practices when they know their performance will be reviewed and disclosed every three months. Reducing this cadence risks weakening oversight and could allow emerging problems to go unnoticed for longer periods.

While I understand the desire to reduce regulatory burdens on public companies, I believe the benefits of quarterly reporting far outweigh the costs. Transparency is the foundation of healthy markets, and any step that reduces it would be a step backward for investors and the public.

Thank you for considering my perspective on this important issue. I urge the Commission to preserve quarterly earnings reporting requirements in the interest of fairness, transparency, and market stability.

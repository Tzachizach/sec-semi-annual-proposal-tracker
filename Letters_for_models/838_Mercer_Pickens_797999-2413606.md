Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Mercer Pickens

Dear Commissioners:

I am writing as an individual investor to express my opposition to Rule S7-2026-15 and any proposal that would reduce the frequency of required corporate financial reporting.

Quarterly financial information is one of the most important tools I use when making investment decisions. Timely reporting allows me to evaluate a company's performance, monitor changes in business conditions, and make informed choices about allocating my capital. Reducing the frequency of required disclosures would leave investors with less information for longer periods of time and would make the markets less transparent.

I am also concerned that longer gaps between required reports would create an unfair advantage for corporate insiders and other parties with access to nonpublic information. Public investors depend on regular disclosures to maintain a level playing field. When reporting intervals become longer, information asymmetries increase, and ordinary investors are placed at a disadvantage.

Some have suggested that companies could simply choose to provide more frequent updates voluntarily. I do not believe this adequately addresses the problem. Companies whose investors most need regular updates, particularly those facing operational challenges, financial uncertainty, or significant business changes, may be the least likely to provide robust voluntary disclosures. Investors should not have to rely on optional reporting to obtain information that is fundamental to making informed investment decisions.

Finally, while reducing reporting requirements may provide some cost savings to issuers, I do not believe those savings outweigh the costs imposed on investors. Transparent, timely financial reporting is a cornerstone of investor confidence and efficient capital markets. Any modest reduction in compliance costs should not come at the expense of market transparency, accountability, and investor protection.

For these reasons, I urge the Commission to reject Rule S7-2026-15 and preserve strong, regular reporting requirements for public companies.

Thank you for considering my comments.

Respectfully submitted,

Mercer Pickens
Seattle, WA

Individual investor: 
It is difficult enough for the middle-class working American to find good information to purchase stocks and ETFs. Large investors, hedge funds, institutions, and brokerages deal with the numbers 24/7. They also control what information is released and when, putting individual investors at a disadvantage. Quarterly filings are a necessity for smaller investors like myself. Even under the current system, transparency is minimal, with items being buried in the details. 

Corporations can sometimes stretch the truth; unfortunately, many do. 

Even now, with the extensive run-up in AI stocks, the actual value of companies would be hidden even further with an extended wait between reporting periods. 

It is the SEC's mandate to protect the market and investors. Limiting public information is no way to accomplish this mandate. Semi-annual reporting does neither. 

I am against relaxing the quarterly reporting requirements. The SEC should require tightening of accounting regulations to ensure full transparency and the highest level of integrity in corporate reporting.

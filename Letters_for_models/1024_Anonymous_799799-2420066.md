Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Anonymous

I am writing as a corporate finance and M&A attorney to highlight a potential issue regarding the proposed shift to semi-annual reporting. In M&A and financing transactions, we often rely heavily on quarterly filings for various diligence points. The shift to semi-annual reporting means we may end up working with older data or requesting supplementary information from companies, which can raise costs and reduce transparency, as information that would otherwise be part of public filings may instead be shared privately/confidentially, potentially increasing the risk of less scrupulous disclosures.  While this issue alone does not seem material enough to stop the proposed rule, it should be considered along with other comments highlighting other problematic consequences of adopting the proposed rule.

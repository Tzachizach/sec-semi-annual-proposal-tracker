Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: James Cornell

As an individual investor for 50 years and member of an investment club for 20 years, I depended heavily on quarterly financial reports both for the financial data as well as for the comments from management about their business and industry.  Semi-annual and annual data does not always show the seasonal trends or early indicators of trouble ahead.  At one time our investment club held shares in a company headquartered in France where only semi-annual financial data was required. Eventually, we felt the lack of transparency was not worth the risk of holding that investment. Please do not place that handicap on investors in US stocks. Keep our public companies as transparent as possible. It will ensure that their shares are regarded in the best possible light - quarterly light.

My name is Alan Birmingham Jr. of Newark, Ohio, and I am a cook at a Hilton hotel. I have been in hospitality and tourism business for twenty years. This is important to me because I realize that regular people have stake in securities, and so they should. Anybody should be able to have a fair shot at trading in securities. 
Cutting quarterly reporting on securities seems irresponsible to me. If anything, we should have more reporting on them. I feel like this is a kind of attempt at shafting less-able people from contending in securities, which would be both devious and immoral. Let us please not do this, and go ahead, retain our previous configuration of quarterly reporting in securities, I think that this is the only fair way to treat everybody, and not just high-dollar people who have MORE stake in the market than everybody else, or anybody else. Only because there aren't more people trading in securities doesn't make it right to make it harder, particularly at a time when the cost of living is higher, to trade in them.

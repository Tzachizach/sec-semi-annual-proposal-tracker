Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Kevin Montalti

Removing the quarterly reporting requirement would allow external auditors to spend more time on the actual year-end audit. As an external auditor, the quarterly reporting requirement acts as a disruption to our overall audit procedures as they stop the team from performing audit procedures for often a month at a time (and thus removing the requirement gives back audit teams two additional months to focus on the audit). More time to focus on the audit will allow for better audit quality, and overall, better less crazy busy seasons for external auditors as year-end work can be pushed up to the interim period - especially in October and November for companies that are a 12/31 filer.

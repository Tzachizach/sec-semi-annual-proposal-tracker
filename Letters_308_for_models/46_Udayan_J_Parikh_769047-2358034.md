As a retail investor, I rely on the quarterly earnings report that are given to me on media by the executives of publicly traded companies. It gives me how the company is performing and what the future holds to my investments. By going to have them go semiannually, it poses risk to investment, as the history shows many companies have gone from top to delisting from stock market in matter of few days. I completely agree that quarterly reporting standards might be overkilled to management of the company and costly; at the same time it is crucial for investors for transperancy. Perhaps better solution is to have Quadrimester (every 4 months) rather than every 3 months. Going semianually has a huge risk as it is unchartered territory and do not know what can go wrong for investors. By going every 4 months, investor still get enough transparency and management can save cost and focus more on running a company that worry about what to report to investor, as it does take quite some management time on preparing for report every 3 months.

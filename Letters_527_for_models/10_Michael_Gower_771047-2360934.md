# Letter 10 — Michael Gower, CPA

- **Date:** 2026-05-07
- **Source:** https://www.sec.gov/comments/S7-2026-15/s7202615-771047-2360934.html

---

Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Michael Gower

The change to semi-annual filing will cause companies to hide critical data from investors. Corporate management needs to provide longer term guidance to investors to move away from the cycle of focusing on short-term results. This is a management issues, not a reporting issue. There is nothing wrong with short-term fluctuations in stock prices. If management provides reasonable long-term guidance, and this is achieved, the investment community will support the company.

We do not need to provide corporations with incentive to "kick the can: (i.e. bad news) down the road. Information needs to be presented timely. Corporations should not be encouraged to hide news because regulations are relaxed (so no one is looking). The proposed change will only benefit unethical companies.

I am strongly opposed to this change.

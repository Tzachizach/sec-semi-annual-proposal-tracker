For many Americans, retirement is not a luxury. It is the reward for decades of hard work, sacrifice, and responsible saving. Millions of workers spend their entire careers contributing to 401(k)s, IRAs, and pension plans with the hope that they will someday be able to retire with dignity and independence. 

Those Americans depend on transparent and trustworthy markets. When public companies are required to disclose less information less frequently, it becomes harder for ordinary people to understand the health of the businesses that ultimately support their retirement savings. At the same time, corporate insiders and large institutions will continue to have far greater visibility into company performance. 

The average American already faces growing challenges in preparing for retirement. Policies that reduce transparency should be viewed with extreme caution because the consequences are borne not by wealthy insiders, but by workers and retirees whose financial security depends on fair and open markets. After a lifetime of work, Americans deserve stronger protections for their retirement savings, not fewer.

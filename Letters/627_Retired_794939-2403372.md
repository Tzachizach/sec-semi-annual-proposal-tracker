Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Retired

Eliminating quarterly statements is a crazy idea.  It would eliminate half the data points from curves we use to evaluate our investments and make the analysis of companies much less accurate, thus increasing the risk of investing.  Financial statements  are essential to understand a company; its business model, its consistency, its risks, the ability to recognize and solve the problems it faces, its financial strength and many more issues.    It is the most reliable method we have for determining the risks of investing in stocks.  Quarterly statements are especially important when studying new investments and young companies.  Eliminating Quarterly statements would make investing riskier and more difficult to follow companies, especially those companies owned for a short time.

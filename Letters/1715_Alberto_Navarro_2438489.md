To Whom It May Concern: 

I am writing to express my opposition to the proposed change to the current reporting framework. 

My central question is: why change something that works? The existing system serves investors well, and I would rather have more information than less. Reducing reporting frequency or scope gives companies a longer window to obscure their problems, their mistakes, and even their successes. Transparency is not a burden to be minimized; it is the foundation of an informed market. 

Greater transparency produces a better-functioning system. When investors can see more, more often, they can make better decisions and hold companies accountable in a timely way. Less frequent disclosure does the opposite, allowing material developments to accumulate out of public view. 

Rather than scaling reporting back, I would encourage the Commission to consider expanding it. If anything, more frequent disclosure would strengthen the integrity of our markets and the confidence investors place in them.

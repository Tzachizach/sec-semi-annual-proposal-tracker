Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Alejandro

I oppose SEC Rule S7-2026-15. Semiannual reporting would leave retail investors blind for long stretches while institutional investors and insiders who have regular management access, paid research, alternative-data tools, and the ability to demand ad-hoc disclosures continue to receive timely information. That informational asymmetry increases the risk insiders can trade ahead of bad news, allows fraud to grow undetected longer, and shifts the modest compliance savings to executives, not the people whose retirement and college savings depend on reliable disclosure. We do not need further corruption to infiltrate our institutions of justice; weakening transparency would make accountability harder, not easier. Please keep quarterly reporting.

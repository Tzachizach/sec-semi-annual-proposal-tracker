Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Matt Norris

I am submitting this comment to declare my absolute opposition to the S7-2026-15 Semiannual Reporting proposal. Frankly, this is a disgusting attempt to strip away market transparency and hand even more unchecked power to corporate insiders.
Reducing public company reporting from three months to six months is not a matter of streamlining or cutting red tape. It is a deliberate move to create a massive information vacuum. While retail investors, regulatory watchdogs, and the general public are left completely in the dark for half the year, corporate executives and well-connected insiders will have free rein to exploit the system. They know exactly what is going on behind closed doors, and this rule gives them a six-month runway to maneuver, trade on non-public knowledge, and shield themselves from immediate accountability.
This is a total betrayal of the public trust. The market is already heavily skewed to favor the wealthy and the well-connected. We need to be demanding more transparency, tighter oversight, and greater corporate accountability. Instead, this proposal gives massive corporations a pass to hide financial mismanagement, internal crises, labor abuses, or environmental liabilities for twice as long. The SEC's core mandate is to protect everyday investors and ensure fair markets, not to act as a shield for the corporate elite who want to operate in the shadows.
Rolling back transparency to benefit insiders at the expense of the public is completely unacceptable. This proposal must be rejected immediately, and quarterly reporting must remain the absolute minimum standard.

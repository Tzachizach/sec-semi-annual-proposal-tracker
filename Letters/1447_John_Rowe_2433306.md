Hello, 

I am writing in response to the proposal set forth by the SEC and the Publicly Listed Companies which they are supposed to monitor for impropriety. This proposal seeks to limit the frequency in which Publicly Listed Companies are obligated to financially disclose reports that accurately detail the state of each companies’ financial solvency allowing investors to infer that external or internal pressures are affecting financial solvency one way or another. This of course becomes even more difficult to do when reducing the frequency in which investors are affordedbinsight into the companies’ finances while allowing much greater opportunity to “game the system” affording a handful of actors acting in bad faith to make themselves a bunch of money at the expense of everyone else involved and will certainly lead to greater losses which investors will be responsible for while none of the bad actors will be held responsible for impropriety. I disagree with moving from once every three months to once every six months and find it shameful and disgusting that the SEC is ostensibly colluding with those who seek to game the system and provide for greater opportunity to steal from investors. 

Thank you, 

John Rowe

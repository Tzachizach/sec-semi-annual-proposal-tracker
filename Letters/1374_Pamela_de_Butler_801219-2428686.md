Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Pamela de Butler

I am a private retail investor who has been investing on my own for approximately 40 years.  I have been grateful for the transition to Edgar for online reports, which I had to previously review in libraries or request directly from companies.  This helped me to review and assess company financial statements along with their corporate leadership on a timely basis.  I have never been a day trader, but I have benefitted from this knowledge and used it to invest and plan for my retirement.  
Now that I am basically retired, I need this information even more to protect my nest egg.  
Therefore, I am extremely distressed with the move to remove the quarterly filing requirement.  Waiting more than 3 months to be able to review a company's performance and projections is too long.  It will expose retail investors, who do not have access to company information that various large investors do, to increased risk and fear of the U.S. stock market.  Less opacity will result in less investment and breed more rampant disinformation.
Therefore, I strongly reject the proposal of S7-2026-15.

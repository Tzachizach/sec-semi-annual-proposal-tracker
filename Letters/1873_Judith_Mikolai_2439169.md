Keep public reporting at every 3 months. 1 and others rely upon it for my financial planning. Oh my gosh, so much can happen in one month or two, especially in this current political climate, let alone waiting for three months! Six months would be way too long to get results each quarters results help determine decision decisions for the next quarter. The financial reports are like a weather report: even though you receive a prediction with the weather forecast, you don't know exactly what happened in the larger region with the weather until you hear the weather report. Again using a weather analogy, if there's some disaster like a forest fire or a tornado predicted, you don't want to wait for six months to hear the results. You want to hear if the disaster struck, how many are affected, what infrastructure is affected, what things look like moving forward.It's really important that the reporting be closer to real time. Private companies are held much more accountable by reporting the results to the public private companies. Don't always have the public best interest in mind, but they are held accountable by these quarterly reports. Keep them accountable. Keep us informed. Keep the reporting. For private companies at three months. Don't extend it to six.

My name is Nabil Alanbar. I am the CTO of a small computer company. In the past 25 years, I have seen data acquisition and throughput speeds increase 100 fold or more. It is now possible to move and computer data faster than we have ever been able to, and with fewer staff manning the machines. It is thus incredible that the SEC is considering reducing reporting requirements. 

The rolling back of investor protections is crazy - in a world where data flows like water, and reporting is easier than ever, it is incredible that y'all are considering reducing reporting requirements. There is no legitimate reason to do this, and it will only perpetuate more scams and Ponzi-esque schemes. Frankly, it smells like cronyism and corruption. 

I know it is hard for the American government these days, but consider doing the right thing - don't reduce transparency and widen the communications gap further than it already is. Again - there is no legitimate reason to do so. 

Nothing good can happen, it can only bad happen.

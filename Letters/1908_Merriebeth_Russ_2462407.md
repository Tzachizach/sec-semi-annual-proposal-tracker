Please keep the quarterly reporting requirements. A lot can change in a month and even moreso in 6 months! Investors deserve to have up to date information when our retirement investments are at stake. Keeping the shorter term of reporting makes fraud and errors less likely and easier to spot. There is NO benefit to investors in this - savings to the companies isn't even that significant. They still have to do their books and reports and update their boards. In my opinion, the only people benefiting from this are the companies. We investors will bear the cost of worse/less information - the people whose 401(k), IRA, retirement funds, etc hold that stock. 
Safeguard the information transparency and keep the quarterly report requirements. 

Merriebeth Russ 
Merrill, WI

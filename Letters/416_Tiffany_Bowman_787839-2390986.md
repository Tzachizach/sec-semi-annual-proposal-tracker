Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Tiffany Bowman

I oppose any effort to reduce public company reporting from quarterly to twice a year.

As a member of the public investing in public markets, I deserve timely access to information about the companies I invest in. Executives, institutional investors, and large financial firms will always have more access and resources than ordinary investors. Reducing required reporting would only widen that gap.

Quarterly reporting helps create accountability and transparency. It gives everyday investors the ability to track performance, understand risks, and make informed decisions with reasonably current information. Going six months without mandatory reporting would make markets less fair and less transparent for the average person.

Public companies benefit from public capital. In return, the public deserves regular disclosure and visibility into how those companies are performing.

Please protect transparency and maintain quarterly reporting requirements.

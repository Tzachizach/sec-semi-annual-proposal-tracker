Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Matt Marino

As an investor, I'm concerned about less visibility into the companies I'm investing in. The quarterly cadence of reporting has been a reliable standard for decades that serves investors of all kinds while not providing an undue burden upon corporations themselves.

Moving to a half year cadence discourages investment, even if it benefits a relatively small subset of the American population. While corporations may benefit in some ways, it also ensures trust is diminished with less visibility and investors potentially looking at investment alternatives to public shares. For the sake of a healthy market and keeping the investor community more informed, the quarterly cadence of reporting should remain in place.

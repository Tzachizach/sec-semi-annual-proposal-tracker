I'm Jennifer Lohmann Hale- a retired RN and retail investor and I rely on quarterly reports to make decisions about my retirement savings. I don’t want to “fly blind” for six months while those with insider info quietly sell their shares before the bad news tanks the stock. Why give more time for potential fraud to grow? Every major modern accounting scandal was enabled by gaps and opacity in financial disclosure. Quarterly filings shorten the window in which fraud can fester before someone outside the company can see it. The supposed savings from less frequent filings provide savings only to the company and its executives. All around, this proposed new reporting rule would be bad for individual investors. Please continue to require quarterly reporting to help keep markets fair, transparent, and honest.


Concerned citizen,

Jennifer Lohmann Hale

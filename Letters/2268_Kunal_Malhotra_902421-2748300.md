Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Kunal Malhotra

I write in opposition to the proposed amendments under File No. S7-2026-15, which would permit public companies to file semiannual reports on Form 10-S in lieu of quarterly reports on Form 10-Q.

As an individual investor who actively monitors holdings across U.S. and international markets, I rely on quarterly disclosure as a primary tool for assessing company performance, risk, and management execution in near-real time. Moving to a six-month reporting cadence would materially widen the information gap between companies and retail investors, particularly during periods of rapid business change (e.g., earnings deterioration, leadership turnover, regulatory exposure) where a six-month lag could mean significant capital is allocated on stale information.

The cost-savings rationale for this proposal does not, in my view, outweigh the disclosure cost to investors. For most reporting companies, the underlying financial close, internal controls, and audit processes operate continuously regardless of filing frequency - the incremental burden of packaging that information quarterly is comparatively small relative to the value quarterly transparency provides the market.

I urge the Commission to retain mandatory quarterly reporting under Form 10-Q and decline to adopt the proposed semiannual alternative.

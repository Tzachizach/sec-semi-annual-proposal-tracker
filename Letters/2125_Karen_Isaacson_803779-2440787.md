Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Karen Isaacson

The existing state of quarterly reporting should be maintained; not only is it a way for the SEC to see that companies are in compliance, but it helps regulated businesses stay on track.  It's easier for a company to plan on quarterly reporting; it's reminiscent of seasonal change, occurs at a regular interval, and it's a smaller amount of data to process.  It's also a smaller amount of data for the SEC to process, making it easier to get through submitted paperwork in a timely fashion.  Increasing the interval between reports means submitted reports will take more time to process, meaning there's the possibility that the agency will get backlogged.  The current scheme should stay in place.

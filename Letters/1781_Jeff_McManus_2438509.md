The secondary market functions most effectively under the bright honest lights of transparency and quarterly reporting delivers. Delayed reporting hides corporate blunders from individual investors and informs insiders instead. Is the SEC protecting investors from corporate nonsense by delaying reporting ? Explain how less, and less timely information would benefit average investors, please. I defy you to assert a single scenario that is improved by delayed reporting. As the gap widens between the upper and middle class, and the ability to move forward is increasingly stymied by inflation, please do not empower the insiders any more than they already are. Please leave quarterly reporting as it is. Thank you.

Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Melody Downie-Dack

To Securities and Exchange Commission,
I oppose the proposed rule change that would allow for semiannual reporting in place of quarterly reporting for companies.  Quarterly reporting of financials to investors and the public is essential for planning, transparency, and efficiency of U.S. capital markets for sectors across the economy.  The stock market may experience more volatility because information will be reported less frequently and potentially more at one time.  Ordinary Americans who have their retirement savings in investment accounts and their financial advisors will have less reliable, timely information to make decisions that might have long-term consequences for the retirements.  In general, less regulation of the stock market and of businesses is not beneficial to the American people.  This is why the SEC was created in the first place, to protect Americans and the U.S. economy from speculation and fraud (which will be more possible with less reporting).  
I oppose these proposed rule changes regarding optional semiannual reporting, respectfully and thoughtfully.  
Melody Downie-Dack

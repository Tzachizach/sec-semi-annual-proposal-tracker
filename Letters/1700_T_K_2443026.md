Jun. 7, 2026

 
It is critical to investors to maintain the current quarterly reporting requirements for public companies.

Once every six months, as proposed, is simply not enough. We tried this once before, between 1955 and 1970. Less transparency came with lower returns and lower valuations.

For an ordinary investor, a return to those days could mean losing a third, or even half, of our life savings.

Going backwards, especially in our current environment, would be a disservice to all.

Thank you for preventing harm to investors, and businesses, by staying with the current reporting requirements for public.

Tony Karian, BSEE, MBA, JD

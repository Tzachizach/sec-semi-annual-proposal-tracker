Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Mike Dattilo

Quarterly reporting data is critical for all investors ranging from institutional investors to retail investors. This data allows them to make well-informed decisions about capital allocation. Maintaining quarterly reporting requirements is crucial for transparency and trust in the US financial markets. I firmly believe that altering the current quarterly disclosure rules would be detrimental to all investors.

That said, if changes are deemed necessary, it is essential that companies continue to provide key financial statements on a quarterly basis. These include the cash flow statement, balance sheet, and income statement. Additionally, investors must receive up-to-date information on the number of shares outstanding, segment revenues, and detailed disclosures regarding any debt or borrowing. While some elements of the current quarterly filings, such as aspects of the 10-Q, may seem redundant, the core financial data mentioned above remain vital for investors' ability to assess a company's health and prospects.

The strength and integrity of the United States economy and its stock market have been built on the reliability of this information investors receive.

The argument that reducing quarterly reporting would benefit smaller companies is wrong. It's precisely these companies that need to report more frequently, as smaller companies undergo more rapid structural changes than large corporations which is why they need to keep investors adequately informed.

In conclusion, I strongly advocate for preserving the existing quarterly reporting obligations. However, if modifications are made, ensuring the continued quarterly disclosure of the financial data listed above is critical to maintaining investor confidence in the United States.

I am writing to express my strong opposition to the Commission’s proposal to make quarterly reporting optional and allow public companies to switch to a semiannual schedule via Form 10-S. 

Reducing disclosure frequency will severely damage the transparency, efficiency, and fairness of the U.S. capital markets. I urge the Commission to reject this change based on three critical concerns: 

Increased Information Asymmetry: A six-month reporting gap is far too long in today’s fast-moving markets. Depriving investors of timely data will widen the gap between corporate insiders and the public, fueling market speculation, rumors, and heightened volatility. 

The Inadequacy of Voluntary Releases: Unstructured, voluntary quarterly press releases on Form 8-K are no substitute for Form 10-Q. They often lack essential financial statements and MD&A disclosures. Furthermore, allowing companies to choose different reporting schedules creates a fragmentation of disclosure quality, destroying industry comparability for analysts and investors. 

No Material Cost Savings or Strategic Benefits: The administrative savings for companies are highly speculative, as lenders and debt covenants will still require quarterly financial tracking. Additionally, there is no empirical evidence that reducing transparency cures corporate "short-termism." Short-termism is a governance issue, not a disclosure issue, and it should not be solved by withholding vital data from the public. 

The SEC’s core mandate is investor protection. Moving to optional semiannual reporting directly compromises this mission and undermines investor confidence. Please withdraw this proposal and maintain the mandatory quarterly reporting framework.

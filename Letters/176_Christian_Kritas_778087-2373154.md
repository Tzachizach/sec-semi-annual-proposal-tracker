Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Christian Kritas

Quarterly financial statements are an invaluable resource to those who rely on public filings to make investment decisions from financial professionals such as financial advisors to amateur investors and students of finance. I am now a financial services professional but started out analyzing annual and quarterly financial statements as a student in my student managed investment fund at university. Being able to analyze and learn from quarterly financial statements was an invaluable part of my financial education in school and is still something I practice as an amateur investors today. To lose such a resource would be a huge blow to those passionate about capital markets everywhere and would put those without access to be expensive private market data at a disadvantage. Releasing quarterly statements can be tedious but there are alternatives available such as Reg D for companies where going fully public does not make sense. I hope those considering repealing the requirement of quarterly filings consider these factors and make the best decisions for all Americans.

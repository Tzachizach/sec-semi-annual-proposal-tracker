Howard Vine, Esq. former head of Corporate Governance for the National Association of Manufacturers. The idea of abbreviating public reporting quarterly will damage the integrity of all corporate governance. Quarterly reporting improves accountability and assures consumers of stock and other investments the most timely information on which to measure corporate competence and essential reporting of problem issues. Failure to mandate quarterly reporting will leave the public with less information than is reasonable to make investment decisions. The SEC has always been the progenitor of corporate responsibility and accountability. Transparency has always kept the marketplace efficient and informed. Allowing corporations to conceal for extended periods matters which will influence corporate behavior and disadvantage public consumers is harmful to the public.  The efficient market hypothesis is based on the fundamental that the share price accurately reflect ongoing issues. Failure to provide timely reporting will allow unforeseen complications to go unnoticed for far too long. Keep the quarterly reporting obligations!!

Howard Vine

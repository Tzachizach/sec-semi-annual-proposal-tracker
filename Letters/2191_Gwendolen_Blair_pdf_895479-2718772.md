Re: File No. $7-2026-15 — Semiannual Reporting
Dear Secretary,
I appreciate the opportunity to comment on the Securities and Exchange Commission's proposed
rule, File No. S7-2026-15, regarding optional semi-annual reporting.
T respectfully oppose the proposal because I believe quarterly reporting remains an essential
component of transparent, fair, and efficient U.S. capital markets.
Quarterly reports provide investors with timely, standardized, and legally accountable
information. While reducing reporting frequency may lower compliance costs for some issuers,
those savings must be weighed against the increased information asymmetry that would result
from fewer mandatory disclosures. Public markets function best when all investors have regular
access to reliable information.
Reducing required reporting from three quarterly reports to one semiannual report would likely
have several unintended consequences:

e Retail investors would receive less timely financial information while institutional
investors would continue to benefit from private research, alternative data, and frequent
management interactions.

« Price discovery could become less efficient as material information accumulates over
longer periods before being reflected in SEC filings.

' «Market volatility may increase around the remaining reporting periods because more
information would be released at once.

¢ Companies facing operational or financial deterioration could remain under reduced
public scrutiny for longer periods.

The proposal is intended, in part, to encourage long-term decision-making. However, short-term
management behavior is driven more by executive compensation, incentive structures, and
market expectations than by the existence of quarterly reporting itself. Reducing transparency
does not necessarily promote better corporate governance.

If the Commission believes reporting requirements should be modernized, I encourage
consideration of more targeted reforms, such as:

¢ simplifying Form 10-Q disclosure requirements,

¢ eliminating duplicative narrative disclosures,

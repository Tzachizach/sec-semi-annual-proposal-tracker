I respectfully urge the Commission to retain mandatory quarterly reporting for U.S. public companies.

Quarterly reporting provides investorsâparticularly individual retail investorsâwith timely, standardized, and reliable information needed to make informed investment decisions. More frequent reporting promotes market transparency, improves price discovery, enhances corporate accountability, and helps identify financial deterioration before it becomes more severe.

While reducing compliance costs is a worthwhile objective, the benefits of quarterly reporting to investor confidence and fair, efficient markets outweigh those costs. Less frequent mandatory reporting could increase information asymmetry between institutional and retail investors, reduce transparency, and delay the public disclosure of material trends affecting shareholders.

I encourage the Commission to preserve the current quarterly reporting framework while continuing to explore targeted ways to reduce unnecessary compliance burdens without sacrificing transparency.

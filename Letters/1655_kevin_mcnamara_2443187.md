Companies still have to keep their books. They still have to brief their boards. The actual savings from skipping a public filing are modest legal and accounting fees, and those savings flow to the company and its executives. The cost of worse information falls on you, the person whose 401(k), IRA, or college fund holds the stock. That's a bad trade for ordinary investors like myself.

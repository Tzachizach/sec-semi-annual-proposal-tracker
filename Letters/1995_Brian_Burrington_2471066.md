To the Securities and Exchange Commission:
Reducing reporting requirements, and any reduction in transparency for investors is a terrible idea. 
For two modern examples of corporate fraud, one doesn't need to look further than FTX and Enron.
In light of catastrophic consequences of these and a myriad other examples, we should be increasing checks and balances, transparency, and most importantly... consequences for corporate fraud.
Who are the victims of corporate fraud? Pension holders, retirees, and the younger generations who are investing in their future. These are your own family, friends, and coworkers who will suffer.
A reduction in reporting requirements will quickly lead to a reduction in confidence in our markets. That is the last thing we want.

Sincerely,
Brian Burrington

To the Securities and Exchange Commission, 

I am writing to formally express my strong opposition to the SEC’s proposal to allow public companies to move from quarterly to semiannual financial reporting. As a retail investor, I believe this change would be detrimental to the integrity of our markets and the safety of individual investors. 

Public financial disclosure is a cornerstone of market transparency. Quarterly reporting is essential because it provides investors with a necessary, timely window into a company's health. Extending this window to six months would leave retail investors "flying blind," creating significant information asymmetry. It would unfairly advantage corporate insiders and institutional investors, who would possess material, non-public information for much longer periods than the general public. 

Furthermore, I am concerned that reducing the frequency of reporting would create more opportunities for corporate misconduct to fester. History has shown that transparency is the best defense against accounting scandals. Lengthening reporting gaps does not improve long-term business strategy; it only increases the potential for fraud and market volatility. 

Finally, while proponents argue that this change reduces compliance burdens, these savings are minimal for the corporations involved and do not justify the risk imposed on the investing public. My retirement savings and financial planning depend on accurate, frequent, and reliable data. I strongly urge the Commission to reject this proposal and maintain the quarterly reporting standard. 

Thank you for your time and for considering the perspective of individual investors. 

Sincerely, 

Michael Johnston 
Enterprise Account Executive

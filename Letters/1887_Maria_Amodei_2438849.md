Jun. 7, 2026

 
I strongly object to reducing visibility into the accounting and financial health of public companies. These companies have a responsibility to all their investors, not just their executive and large institutional investors. Most companies will elect to file every 6 months, saving relatively modest filing costs and allowing them to conceal real problems while representing financial health to investors. Lack of visibility allows companies to artificially attract investment, hurting investors, particularly many small investors, which in turn hurts the economy overall while some few might benefit. Skip the BS that honor and altruism will prevail. 

Maria Amodei, Senior Data Engineer

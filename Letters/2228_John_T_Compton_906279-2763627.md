Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: John T. Compton

I spent many years on Wall Street as an analyst, an investment banker, and a bond trader. Markets thrive on information and transparency. The more you know about a company in real time, the better your ability to assess its financial health and potential. To abandon quarterly reporting---the gold standard---in favor of semi-annual reporting would assault and diminish these principles, making it much harder for investors to assess risk and allocate investment capital. The system works very well as it is. Why risk market turmoil and uncertainty? Why choose opacity over transparency? Why remove critical information from a robust market ecosystem? I urge the SEC to leave the reporting regulations as they are. It isn't broken, so there's no need to fix it.

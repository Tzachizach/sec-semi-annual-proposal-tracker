My name is Ashley and I am a project manager in technology. 

I am concerned about proposals that would reduce the frequency of financial disclosures by public companies.
First, timely and consistent disclosure is a cornerstone of transparent and efficient capital markets. Investors rely on regular financial reporting to assess company performance, allocate capital appropriately, and make informed decisions. Extending the period between required disclosures could reduce transparency and increase information asymmetry between company insiders and the investing public.
Second, markets today react rapidly to new information. Less frequent reporting may increase uncertainty and contribute to larger price swings when material information is eventually released. More regular disclosures help investors incorporate information gradually and support more accurate price discovery.
Third, reducing reporting frequency may create additional opportunities for speculation and increase the importance of unofficial signals, rumors, and selective interpretations of company performance. Strong disclosure requirements help promote accountability and investor confidence while reducing the risk of market manipulation and insider advantages.
Quarterly reporting requirements have served investors and markets for decades. As businesses evolve and markets become increasingly interconnected and information-driven, maintaining robust disclosure standards remains important. Rather than reducing transparency, policymakers should focus on ensuring timely reporting, effective enforcement of existing regulations, and equal access to material information for all investors.
For these reasons, I support maintaining the current requirement for public companies to provide financial disclosures every three months.
Thank you.

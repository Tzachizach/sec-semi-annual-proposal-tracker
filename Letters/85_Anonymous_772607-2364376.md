Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Anonymous

I am very much against this proposal. In an environment where there are numerous allegations of insider trading, whether that be in equity markets, crypto, or new "prediction platforms", I believe that the last thing needed is another opportunity for such activity to happen. Given that one can speculate on various outcomes of publicly traded companies within these new and loosely regulated "prediction platforms" only compounds the potential problems such a "withdrawal of transparency requirements" might entail. Quarterly reporting is the absolute minimum standard of transparency for a publicly traded company, and companies that want the privilege to seek public investment should be obligated to at least promptly and methodically release their earnings every three months and explain their performance and future plans to investors.

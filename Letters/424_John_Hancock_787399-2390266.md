Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: John Hancock

To the SEC Commissioners:

I am an attorney employed by a large insurance company and asset management firm. Our organization sponsors offerings registered on Forms S-1, S-3, N-1A, N-2, and N-4.

Please accept the following comments regarding the proposal to transition from quarterly Exchange Act reports to semiannual reporting.

While I recognize that certain reporting obligations impose unnecessary burdens on issuers, I do not believe that reducing the frequency of transparency is the appropriate solution. Instead, I recommend requiring issuers to prepare their management discussion and analysis and provide key performance indicators, including earnings, on a quarterly basis. More comprehensive disclosures could then be provided on a semi-annual schedule. This approach would reduce burdens while preserving timely insight for investors.

I would also like to address the broader discussion around revitalizing the initial public offering market. Although efforts to reduce regulatory burdens are helpful, the most impactful step to make IPOs more attractive would be to raise the accredited investor standard. Because inflation has significantly eroded the value of that threshold over more than forty years, there is now ample private capital available. As a result, many issuers no longer need to access the public markets. Additionally, permitting retail oriented investment funds to hold substantial private assets further reduces the necessity of going public. Today, companies are driven to pursue IPOs primarily by elevated valuations fueled by the scarcity of offerings and favorable market conditions and mostly as a windfall to those private market investors.

Thank you for your consideration.

Sincerely,
John Hancock

P.S. I am not affiliated with John Hancock/ManuLife.

Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: John Flynn Jr.

I am writing to express my strong opposition to the proposal that would allow U.S. public companies to report earnings on a semiannual basis instead of quarterly.

Quarterly reporting is one of the few mechanisms that ensures timely transparency, market discipline, and early detection of financial stress. Reducing reporting frequency would:

Increase information asymmetry between insiders and the investing public
Delay the visibility of deteriorating fundamentals, allowing problems to compound before investors can react
Reduce accountability for management teams who already face limited scrutiny between earnings cycles
Increase volatility, as markets would be forced to digest twice as much information at once
Weaken U.S. market leadership, which has historically been built on rigorous disclosure standards

The argument that quarterly reporting creates "short-termism" is overstated. Companies already have tools to guide long-term expectations without reducing transparency. Investors, analysts, and regulators rely on quarterly data to monitor risk, capital allocation, and operational performance. Semiannual reporting would remove a critical safeguard.

For these reasons, I urge the Commission to withdraw or substantially reconsider this proposal. The costs to market integrity and investor protection far outweigh any claimed benefits.

Thank you for considering this comment.
John Flynn Jr.

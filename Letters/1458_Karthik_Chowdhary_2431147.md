I don't think this is a good decision. Quarterly reporting should remain in place. 

As investors, we rely on regular financial updates to understand how companies are performing and to make informed decisions about the future. Reducing the frequency of reporting would decrease transparency at a time when transparency is more important than ever. 

We're currently in an environment where companies are making massive AI and infrastructure investments, spending billions in capital expenditures. Investors deserve to see the progress, returns, and effectiveness of those investments on a timely basis. Quarterly reports provide an important checkpoint that helps markets evaluate whether management is executing on its strategy. 

Less frequent reporting may reduce accountability and make it harder for shareholders to assess performance. The current quarterly system strikes a good balance between transparency and long-term planning, and I don't see a compelling reason to change it.

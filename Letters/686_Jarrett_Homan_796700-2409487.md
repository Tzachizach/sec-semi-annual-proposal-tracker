Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Jarrett Homan

Dear Commissioners,

I strongly oppose proposed rule S7-2026-15, which allows companies the option to report their finances semiannually instead of quarterly. As a retail investor, I rely on frequent, quarterly updates to make informed decisions for both my personal and retirement investment accounts. Halving our access to this information threatens market transparency.

This rule gives corporate insiders an unfair advantage. Six months of silence provides executives ample time to quietly sell their stock before the public discovers bad news. Furthermore, doubling the time between reports helps bad actors conceal financial ruin, whereas quarterly audits expose problems early and prevent fraud from festering. While proponents claim this rule reduces corporate paperwork, retail investors pay the actual price by navigating the market blind.

Even if the SEC labels this change voluntary, opaque reporting will quickly become the new normal. Once major companies adopt semiannual reporting, others will inevitably follow suit. We know this system fails because international attempts have already caused severe stock volatility and massive frauds, forcing many countries to return to the quarterly standard.

The SEC exists to protect investors, not to help companies conceal their performance. Please defend market transparency and maintain the quarterly reporting standard.

Very Respectfully,

Jarrett Homan

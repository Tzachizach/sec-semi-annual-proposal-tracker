Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Michelle Beason

Dear Securities and Exchange Commission,

I am writing to express my strong support for maintaining the current requirement that all publicly traded companies file quarterly financial reports (Form 10-Q). I urge the Commission to reject any proposals that would reduce or eliminate this reporting obligation.

Quarterly reporting is a cornerstone of market transparency and investor protection. It ensures that shareholders and the public have timely, accurate information about a company's financial health, enabling informed investment decisions. Reducing this requirement to semi-annual or annual reporting would create significant information gaps that could expose investors to undue risk and undermine confidence in U.S. capital markets.

Furthermore, quarterly disclosures serve as an important check on corporate management, encouraging accountability and reducing the likelihood of financial misconduct going undetected for extended periods. The integrity of our markets depends on this consistent flow of information.

I strongly encourage the SEC to uphold quarterly reporting standards for all companies listed on U.S. stock exchanges and to resist pressure to weaken these critical investor protections.

Thank you for your consideration of my comments.

To the SEC regarding File # S7-2026-15: 


Colleagues and I are members of an investment club that is part of the National Association of Investment Clubs (NAIC). We are opposed to the SEC proposal to reduce the number of certified financial filings each year by half. That is not what we want as investors. The importance of having these filings reviewed by independent auditors and then signed by the CEO and CFO of our holdings cannot be understated. These quarterly filings are critical to our ability to make better decisions in choosing which companies we invest in, and even more importantly, the timing of our purchase decisions and sales decisions during each and every quarter of the year. We value and trust these quarterly filings and along with our own research and analyses, they form the basis/guidelines for those decisions. 


Our club meets monthly for three hours to review the stocks in our portfolio. Waiting six months for every financial filing would be cumbersome and make our investment decisions extremely difficult to complete in a timely manner. We have "been in business" as an investment club for over 30 years, and we know the value of making purchases and sales decisions in a timely manner. Without quarterly certified filings, how can we make the most thoughtful and decisive decisions with any confidence? 


This proposed change not only impacts our club, but many of our club members are also private investors. We are funding our retirements and the education of our children and grandchildren with our investments. Why would the SEC want to make this more difficult for us? We truly believe in the companies we support; please reject this proposed change and support the timely and accurate quarterly reporting of every company's efforts. 


Sincerely yours, 
Barbara Garrison 
Financial Analyst 
Abundance Strategist Investment Club 
Member NAIC

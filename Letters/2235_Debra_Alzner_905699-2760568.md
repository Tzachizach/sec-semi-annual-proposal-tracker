Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Debra Alzner

As an individual investor, I am opposed to reporting requirements changing from quarterly to semi-annually. In this day and age, information should be available in a more timely, not less timely manner. Data analytics are pervasive throughout companies. As an individual whose profession is in data analytics, it is unconscionable to me that we would provide people with less timely information when corporate executives are always clamoring for quicker access to information. Insiders already have an advantage and it is not appropriate for individual investors to be at even more of a disadvantage information wise by moving public reporting from quarterly to semi-annually. Companies still have to keep their General Ledger software up to date and they still have to brief their boards. So, the information is available and to keep the markets fair this information needs to be provided to ordinary investors including 401K, 403b, 457, IRA, 529, and other ordinary investors! Instead of making the information less timely, the SEC should be investigating how information can be made available even more timely than it already is. Technology exists for a reason. Leverage it!

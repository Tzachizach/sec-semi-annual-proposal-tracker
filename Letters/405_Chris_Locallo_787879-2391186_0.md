Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Chris Locallo

I appreciate the opportunity to comment on the Commission's proposal to transition from quarterly to semiannual reporting. While I understand the intent to reduce compliance burdens, I believe the change would introduce significant risks to market transparency and investor protection.

Quarterly reporting provides a consistent flow of information that allows the public to monitor performance trends, identify emerging risks, and evaluate management decisions with reasonable continuity. Extending the interval to six months increases the opacity of corporate operations and heightens the likelihood that material issues will go undetected for extended periods.

This information gap would not be experienced equally. Insiders and sophisticated market participants often have access to alternative channels of insight - industry data, supply-chain signals, private communications, and other nonâpublic indicators - that allow them to anticipate developments well before the public disclosures. As a result, semiannual reporting would widen information asymmetries and increase the probability of abrupt, outsized market reactions when disclosures finally occur.

These dynamics undermine the Commission's longstanding commitment to fair, orderly, and efficient markets. For these reasons, I respectfully urge the Commission to retain the quarterly reporting framework to ensure that all investors have timely access to material information.

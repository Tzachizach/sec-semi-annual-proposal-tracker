To the Securities and Exchange Commission: 

I am submitting this comment regarding File No. S7-2026-15, the Commission’s proposal concerning semiannual reporting by public companies. 
I respectfully oppose any rule change that would allow public companies to replace quarterly reporting with semiannual reporting. 
Regular quarterly reporting is an important part of the transparency that makes U.S. public markets trustworthy. Investors rely on Form 10-Q reports to understand a company’s financial condition, operating results, liquidity, risks, and business trends throughout the year. Reducing required reporting from four times per year to two times per year would leave investors with less timely information and longer periods of uncertainty. 
This change would especially harm ordinary retail investors. Large institutions may have greater access to management, private research, alternative data, and market intelligence. Retail investors, by contrast, rely heavily on public disclosures filed with the SEC. If companies are allowed to report less often, the information gap between sophisticated market participants and the general investing public will grow. 
Form 8-K filings are not an adequate substitute for quarterly reports. Current reports may disclose certain material events, but they do not provide the full financial statements, management discussion, liquidity analysis, and broader context that investors receive in a Form 10-Q. Investors need consistent, standardized reporting, not a patchwork of event-based disclosures. 
Optional semiannual reporting would also make it harder to compare companies. If some companies continue to report quarterly while others report only twice a year, investors will have less consistent information across industries and peer groups. This would reduce comparability and could make markets less efficient. 
I understand concerns that quarterly reporting can contribute to short-term thinking. However, reducing transparency is not the right solution. Long-term investing is supported by better information, not less information. Investors can evaluate long-term strategy more effectively when they have regular, reliable updates about a company’s actual performance. 
The SEC’s mission includes protecting investors, maintaining fair and efficient markets, and facilitating capital formation. Mandatory quarterly reporting supports those goals. Allowing companies to provide less frequent financial disclosure would weaken investor protection and reduce confidence in the fairness of the market. 
For these reasons, I urge the Commission not to adopt the proposed semiannual reporting rule. Public companies should continue to be required to file quarterly reports so that all investors have timely, consistent, and comparable information. 

Respectfully submitted, 

Ken

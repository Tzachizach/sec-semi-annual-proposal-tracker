Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Lisa Hubbs

Having lived through the bankong crash of 2008, the panic of 1999 heading into the new millenium, and so many volatile times, those quarterly reports were the only vehicle that maintained investor faith in a system to either uphold or crash our fragile economic system. Without quarterly reporting, there is zero trust that the industry as a whole will do upstanding measures to keep this country running. Nothing. 
We are talking heading towards 1929 all over again. Investors put hard earned dollars in companies they trust. No reporting = no trust.

The SEC members should be ashamed, and all should be relinquished from their positions who vote for this measure. Only those who wish to do unscrupulous things would approve of this. as it protects the rich few in power and leaves the vast majority of Americans investing in what hard little earned dollars they have for an even more uncertain future. How dare you even consider this. 

I personally will strongly consider taking my assets and relocating them to either companies who choose to retain quarterly reporting or out completely.

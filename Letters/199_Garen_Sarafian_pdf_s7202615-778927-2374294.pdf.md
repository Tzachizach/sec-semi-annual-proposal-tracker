Re: Optional Semiannual Reporting for Public Companies
Dear Ms. Countryman:
Bausch Health Companies Inc. (NYSE:BHC)(TSX:BHC) ("Bausch Health") appreciates the opportunity to
comment on the Securities and Exchange Commission’s proposed amendments to permit optional
semiannual reporting on new Form 10-S in lieu of quarterly reporting on Form 10-Q.
We support the Commission’s effort to provide greater flexibility in the public company reporting
framework and recognize the value of revisiting long-standing requirements as investor and issuer needs
evolve.
That said, we believe that a change in reporting frequency alone would be unlikely to materially reduce
overall reporting effort, staffing levels, or compliance costs. At best, the proposal would result in a
modest reduction in resource demands.
Potential Benefits
We acknowledge several incremental benefits associated with the proposal. Eliminating the Form 10-Q
filing process for two fiscal quarters per year could reduce administrative burden and modestly lower
external auditor quarterly review procedures and related fees. Registrants electing semiannual reporting
may also experience some reduction in legal and disclosure drafting work tied specifically to non-filed
quarters, along with limited simplification of certain SEC reporting processes.
Practical Constraints Limiting Resource Reduction
In practice, we would expect to continue a quarterly financial close, earnings, and disclosure cadence
regardless of an election to file semi-annual reports. Investor expectations, lender requirements, Board
oversight, and internal operational needs continue to drive quarterly reporting discipline. Internal
quarterly reports, presentations, review meetings, external-ready talking points, possible related Forms
8-Ks, and other supporting materials would therefore likely remain necessary. In addition, the associated
legal, finance, and disclosure review would continue.

As a result, the core finance, accounting, internal controls, and reporting infrastructure required to
produce timely and reliable quarterly information would remain largely unchanged. Within the
pharmaceutical industry, peer companies may also elect to continue quarterly reporting practices,
reinforcing market expectations for quarterly transparency and comparability.
Implications for Staffing and Cost
Given these dynamics, we believe the proposal would translate into no more than a modest reduction in
reporting effort or cost. The change in filing frequency alone would not meaningfully alter staffing
requirements across finance, accounting, legal, or investor relations functions.
For semiannual reporting to have a more significant impact on resources, broader structural changes
would likely be necessary, such as extended filing deadlines and/or streamlined reporting requirements
similar to certain European reporting frameworks. Absent such changes, the practical demands of
quarterly financial communication are likely to persist.
Conclusion
We appreciate the Commission’s efforts to consider greater flexibility for registrants through this
proposal. However, we believe that the proposed amendments, as currently designed, are unlikely to
result in meaningful reductions in reporting resources or costs. Any material impact would likely require
a more comprehensive reengineering of interim reporting requirements.
We thank the Commission for its consideration of our views and for the opportunity to comment.

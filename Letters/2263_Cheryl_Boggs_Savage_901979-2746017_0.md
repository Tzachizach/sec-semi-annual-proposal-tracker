Europe has semiannual reporting, as is reported to be being proposed here, by the US Securities and Exchange Commission. However, Europe's public company FinRept is "Principles Based,"" and their countries' leadership is far more participatively involved and invested in regulatory oversight of their financial markets.  (And far less susceptible to their political favoritism being bought and paid for than ours is, by way of our fast and loose campaign finance permissiveness - we've basically LEGALIZED bribing our pols!)

US Accounting is "Rules Based," with millions of them awarding Special Treatment allowances which allow the DEPARTURE FROM FOUNDATIONAL ACCOUNTING PRINCIPLES for certain transactions and industries. Again: millions of them! 

Quarterly reporting is therefore crucial in our case. As is far MORE regulatory oversight than our government is presently interested in, apparently. Therefore I view the SEC's reported present endeavor to undertake a reduction from 4 financial statement filings (3 forms 10-Q, for companies' quarters 1-3, and then their annual 10-K) to 2 as an imminent threat to the stability of our financial markets.

## SUMMARY OF THE LINKED FORBES ARTICLE (summarized for the tracker; not the original text)

Her SEC comment links to her Forbes article "The Hidden Risk of Less Disclosure: Why Banks Need Quarterly Reporting" (2026-05-06). In my own words:

She opposes the optional-semiannual proposal as applied to banks. Banking runs on confidence, so anything that increases opacity is dangerous. The largest banks will stay quarterly; the consequential cohort is mid-sized regional banks (roughly $20-150B in assets), which face real cost pressure and thinner analyst coverage and are the most likely to opt in, and that is exactly where transparency is already most fragile. Under semiannual reporting, gradual credit deterioration and deposit outflows stay invisible until they surface all at once, turning what would have been incremental repricing into a single shock; in a digitally connected system (the 2023 SVB collapse is her reference point) that can trigger rapid deposit flight and contagion to peer institutions, especially other semiannual reporters that investors cannot easily distinguish. She also flags a "signaling trap": once some banks switch, the choice itself signals lower transparency or higher risk, so stronger banks keep quarterly while switchers face higher funding costs, stratifying the system. She urges regulators, investors, credit-rating analysts, and banks to file comments.

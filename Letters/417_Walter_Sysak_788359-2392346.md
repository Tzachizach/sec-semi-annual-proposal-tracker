Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Walter Sysak

I am writing as a retail investor to oppose the proposal allowing companies to file semiannual reports on Form 10-S in lieu of quarterly reports on Form 10-Q.
Markets move faster every year, not slower. Cutting public reporting in half does not reduce the information a company generates. Books will still be closed monthly. This only widens the knowledge gap between insiders and retail investors. Disclosure rules exist to narrow that gap.
Longer gaps between filings also concentrate price-moving information into fewer events, resulting in increased volatility. Retail investors, who depend almost entirely on public filings, will bear the brunt of that volatility.
The Commission's investor-protection mandate should weigh more heavily than the filing-cost relief this proposal offers. To preserve market stability and the confidence of everyday investors, I urge the Commission to maintain the mandatory quarterly reporting schedule.

# Letter 36 — Matthew Truty

- **Date:** 2026-05-06
- **Role/Affiliation:** Individual investor and compliance professional
- **Stance:** Oppose
- **Source:** https://www.sec.gov/comments/S7-2026-15/s7202615-769467-2358654.html

---

I write this as an individual investor and compliance professional that stands strongly against this proposal. Although we see elite investors like Warren Buffett and Jamie Dimon comment how quarterly reporting has "contributed to the decline in the number of public companies in America over the past two decades," of what relevance is this to the individual investor, to which the Commission is responsible for protecting? On the SEC's website, the mission statement is clear that its pillars are protecting investors, maintaining fair, orderly and efficient markets, and facilitating capital formation.

I don't believe it's any coincidence that the protection of investors is the number one priority listed in the mission statement, and here's a great opportunity to assert that.

Lastly, does the expansion of publicly traded companies make up for the possibility of an Enron-like disaster repeating? Is $60B in investor losses a sufficient tradeoff for more lax reporting standards? Why give publicly traded companies any more opportunity to dupe the individual investor?

Let's prioritize what helps American citizens and the retail investor, not the agenda of the elite investor.

Thank you for your time and consideration.

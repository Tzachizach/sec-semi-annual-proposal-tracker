Quarterly SEC reporting should be preserved to ensure robust transparency and accountability in our capital markets. Frequent, timely disclosures give investors critical visibility into a company’s financial health, performance, and risks, enabling better-informed decisions. Moving to biannual reporting would reduce oversight, create dangerous information gaps, and weaken corporate discipline by allowing problems to build unchecked for longer periods. Strong transparency through quarterly filings strengthens market integrity, protects investors, and promotes long-term economic confidence. 

It is beyond important that financial situational awareness is maintained at its current schedule. Longer terms of silence will result in larger problems once revealed.

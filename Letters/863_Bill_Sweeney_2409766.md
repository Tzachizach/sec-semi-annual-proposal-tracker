Insider information is already an advantage to companies who are going public.    Allowing them to conceal even more data can only hurt investors not directly associated with the company.

The scenarios where hiding the information is an unfair advantage are:
- the company growth has exploded over the last two quarters.   Investors  receive a skewed view of further growth potential.
- the company growth has significantly diminished or leveled off.   Investors see only the prior growth periods not the slowing.
-  the underwriters will have insight into the data hidden from the investing public.   This will allow them to offload the stock if they know that it will likely crash &/or over invest in the stock if they see growth taking off
- in this day of building models to forecast return,  example using AI to predict growth, data is king.   By allowing insiders to control access to the data you are giving them a significant market advantage in model building.

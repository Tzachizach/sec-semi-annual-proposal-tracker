### Draft Comment for [Submit Comments on S7-2026-15](https : / / www . sec . gov / comments / s7-2026-15 / semiannual-reporting#no-back)

As a Sales Director, financial literacy coach, and active individual investor, I am writing to express my strong opposition to the proposal to shift from quarterly to semiannual reporting. In my role coaching others on financial literacy, I constantly emphasize that timely data is the bedrock of smart, confident investing. Personally, I rely extensively on Form 10-Q filings every single quarter to evaluate company performance, track operational health, and cross-reference financial statements against management's stated goals. Waiting six months for a single update severely diminishes an individual investor's ability to make agile, informed decisions.

Moving to a semiannual reporting structure significantly degrades corporate transparency and weakens our ability to keep tabs on C-suite behavior. A six-month window creates a dangerous information vacuum where corporate mismanagement, shifting risk profiles, or poor strategic decisions can be obscured for far too long. Reducing the frequency of these disclosures opens the door to unethical corporate behavior, as executives would face far less immediate accountability to the public.

Ultimately, eroding the frequency of these vital public filings will only breed widespread doubt in the integrity of the stock market. Transparency is what builds investor confidence; withholding information for longer periods will inevitably lead to market volatility and a erosion of trust. To protect the retail investing public and ensure market integrity, the SEC must maintain the current quarterly reporting framework.

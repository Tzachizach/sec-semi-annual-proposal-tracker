Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: William Cronk

To Whom It May Concern,

I am writing to express my strong opposition to any proposal that would reduce the frequency of public company reporting from quarterly to semiannual. Such a change would significantly weaken market transparency and disproportionately disadvantage smaller, everyday investors who rely on timely information to make sound financial decisions.

Quarterly reporting provides a consistent flow of data that helps level the playing field. Large institutional investors already have access to sophisticated analytics, direct company engagement, and alternative data sources. Retail investors, by contrast, depend heavily on standardized, SEC-mandated disclosures to understand a companyâs performance, risks, and trajectory. Extending the reporting cycle to six months would widen the information gap between those with privileged access and those without it.

Longer periods without required disclosures also increase the likelihood that material issues - such as deteriorating financial conditions, governance failures, or emerging risks - remain hidden until they become far more damaging. Quarterly reporting acts as an early-warning mechanism that protects smaller investors from being blindsided.

Additionally, the argument that quarterly reporting is overly burdensome is increasingly outdated. Modern technology, including AI-driven financial tools, has dramatically reduced the cost and complexity of preparing accurate disclosures. AI can automate reconciliation, detect anomalies, streamline narrative drafting, and ensure compliance with reporting standards. These advancements make more frequent, higher-quality reporting not only feasible but efficient.

For the protection of retail investors and the continued integrity of U.S. capital markets, I urge the SEC to maintain the current quarterly reporting requirement.

Thank you for your consideration.

Sincerely,

William Cronk

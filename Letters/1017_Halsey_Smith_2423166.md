You should not change company quarterly financial reporting rules because it opens the door to improper behavior by insiders and it places individual and other investors at a great disadvantage. Being a retiree, I am dependent on my investments for income and I need the most current information in order to make rational investment decisions. Changing these rule will place all investors at an unfair disadvantage; particularly if you do not place very strict trading restrictions on all insiders, e.g., board members, employees, business partners, and others who have non-public information - including their family members.

Information disclosure, such as financials, lawsuits, new products, product issues and the such are extremely necessary for determining the appropriateness of making an investment or selling an investment. That information should be regularly available and on a timely basis for all investors; particularly smaller investors who have limited access to information beyond company reports.

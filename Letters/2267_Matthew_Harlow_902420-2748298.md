Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Matthew Harlow

I am writing to express my strong opposition to the SEC's proposed rule, File No. S7-2026-15, which would replace quarterly Form 10-Q filings with semiannual Form 10-S filings. As a retail investor, I believe this change would severely harm market transparency, disadvantage individual investors, and compromise the core mission of the Commission to protect investors and maintain fair, orderly, and efficient markets . Eliminating quarterly reporting will significantly widen the information gap between institutional insiders and ordinary retail investors. Wall Street firms possess the resources to conduct proprietary research and maintain direct access to corporate management. Retail investors, however, rely heavily on the regular, standardized disclosures provided by Form 10-Q to make informed financial decisions . Furthermore, moving to a six-month reporting cycle introduces substantial risks to market integrity. A longer period between public disclosures increases the likelihood of insider trading, as corporate insiders will hold material, non-public information for much longer windows. It will also likely result in extreme, concentrated market volatility when financial data is finally released twice a year, rather than allowing the market to smoothly price in corporate performance every 90 days . While reducing regulatory burdens for corporations is a notable point of discussion, it should never come at the expense of investor protection and market integrity. Frequent transparency is the bedrock of trust in the U.S. financial system.I urge the Commission to reject proposal S7-2026-15 and maintain mandatory quarterly reporting. Thank you for your time and consideration of my comments. Sincerely, Matt Harlow

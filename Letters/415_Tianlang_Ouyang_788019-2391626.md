Subject: File No. S7-2026-15

I am writing to express my strong opposition to the SECâs proposal to make quarterly reporting optional.
As a market participant, I rely heavily on the regular cadence of quarterly disclosures to evaluate corporate performance, assess risk, and make informed investment decisions. Extending the reporting window to six months creates a massive information vacuum. This long gap inherently favors corporate insiders, who will maintain real-time visibility into operations, while leaving public investors to trade on stale, outdated information-creating an uneven playing field that undermines market fairness.
Furthermore, making this rule "optional" does not mitigate its risks. The companies most likely to opt out of quarterly reporting are often the ones facing operational challenges or undergoing transitions-precisely the issuers whose investors need frequent, transparent updates the most.
While reducing regulatory burdens is a notable goal, any administrative savings a company might achieve by moving to a semiannual schedule are negligible compared to the increased cost of capital, heightened volatility, and loss of investor confidence that will result. The minimal savings to corporations are simply not worth the substantial risk and cost imposed on the investing public.
I urge the Commission to withdraw this proposal and maintain the quarterly reporting framework that forms the bedrock of our market's integrity.

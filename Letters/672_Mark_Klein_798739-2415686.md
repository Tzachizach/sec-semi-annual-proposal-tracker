Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Mark Klein

I am absolutely against the proposed semiannual reporting.  Google AI sums up several reasons:  1. Less transparency---investors would have to wait six months instead of three to get official, audited data on a company's financial health; 2. Bigger surprises---with longer gaps between official updates, missing an earnings target could cause massive, volatile swings in stock prices; 3. Advantage for Insiders---large institutional investors can afford alternative data (like tracking satellite images of parking lots or credit card data) to see how a company is doing, while everyday retail investors are left in the dark; 4. Harder to Spot Fraud---quarterly checks make it easier for auditors and regulators to catch accounting red flags early before they snowball into major corporate collapses.  Charles Schwab (brokerage) also makes a couple additional points:  1. People generally dislike change---moving to a twice-a-year cadence could lead to less overall engagement from retail investors who look forward to earnings season like a recurring golf or tennis tournament.  Less engagement means larger moves when reports do come out and people/models are triggered to buy/sell; 2. Disrupted data models---a shift to biannual filings would significantly alter backtesting models. Analysts and day traders rely on predictable historical quarterly data to evaluate and prove their trading strategies.  The last point is huge for me as I've done lots of backtesting over my trading/investing career.  It will take years to general a large sample size of data points to see how semiannual reporting affects the landscape.  Even with quarterly reporting, much goes on that escapes the guise/awareness of retail investors.  I can only think semiannual reporting will make this much worse.  Thanks for your time!

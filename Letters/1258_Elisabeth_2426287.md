I am a retail investor and strongly support keeping quarterly reporting requirements for public companies. Quarterly reports are one of the few standardized, reliable sources of information available equally to all investors. Reducing mandatory reporting to every six months would increase information gaps, make it harder for ordinary investors to evaluate companies, and create greater advantages for insiders and large institutions with access to alternative sources of information. The benefits of transparency, accountability, and investor confidence outweigh any compliance burden associated with quarterly reporting. Please retain the current quarterly reporting framework.
Sincerely, 
Elisabeth Hass 
Portland, Oregon

Dear SEC members,

I am a retired management veteran with experience working in several publicly traded technology companies. My wife is a retired medical doctor. Our retirement income is based in large part on the stock holdings in our IRA accounts.

In my opinion, based on career and investment experience, changing to semi-annual corporate reporting is not a good idea. The rise and fall of large companies now happens at the fastest pace ever. As an investor, I want the public information I am depending on to manage my investments to be as current as possible. This was also true when I was stock, options, and 401K holding employee.

While quarterly reports involve considerable resources to prepare, the ongoing improvements in financial management software have made the process increasingly efficient. It is highly likely that AI-assisted tools will expedite processes further. To me, moving to require less frequent reporting at this time seems counterintuitive.

Thank you for your consideration.

Sincerely,
John A. Stark

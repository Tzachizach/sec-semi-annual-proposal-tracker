I am against this change because it will be detrimental to shareholders who will not be as well and up to date informed. It has not worked out for glabally and it will open opportunities for businesses and lawyers to know problems in their companies that are still trading with no obligation to inform shareholders until reporting time.
“ The SEC keeps citing the "global standard," but that standard isn't doing well in the places it was tried. The EU's lighter regime is where Wirecard's €1.9 billion fraud hid in plain sight for years before the company collapsed in 2020. The UK's 2014 shift stripped analyst coverage from mid-cap stocks, and London has been bleeding IPOs ever since. Singapore moved to semiannual reporting in 2020 and is now reconsidering, because trading volumes fell and disclosure quality dropped. The "global standard" the SEC is invoking isn't standard, isn't working, and is being walked back where it was tried.” As a citizen and someone who has been an investor, I absolutely do not want the reporting requirements to change.
Susan Corman
Falmouth, Ma

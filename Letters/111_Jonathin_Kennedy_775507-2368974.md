Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Jonathin Kennedy

I am writing to formally express my opposition to the recent SEC proposal that would allow public companies to elect semi-annual reporting instead of the current quarterly requirement.

In a healthy democracy, market transparency is a cornerstone of investor protection. Reducing the frequency of financial disclosures creates significant "dark periods," increasing information asymmetry and potentially favoring institutional insiders over individual retail investors. Rather than moving toward less frequent reporting, I believe the Commission should explore ways to enhance operational transparency-potentially through more frequent or real-time disclosures-to ensure all investors have access to timely, high-quality information.

I urge the SEC to maintain the quarterly reporting standard to preserve the integrity and transparency of our capital markets.

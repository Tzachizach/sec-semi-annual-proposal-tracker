Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Andy Cross

Strongly encourage the SEC to continue the practice of quarterly reporting for all sized companies. Information and data is the lifeblood of investing. Individual investors, let alone professional money managers and institutions, who are real owners of companies, need to hear from the companies they own. We need to see the financial numbers. We need to hear the context. We need to understand from our management teams and see how our capital is invested and to what end. Quarterly reporting helps with this. Removing it, or granting permission to lesson the frequency of reporting, eliminates our access to information and could grant advantages to larger players in the market who already have significant resources and advantages (along with some disadvantages, too). Our focus on long-term investing does not mean we blindly put tuck our heads down and pay not attention to quarterly results. It's a time check for us to gain insights that we can use to make insightful, purposeful, informed decisions. Do not take this flow of oxygen away from us. Please. Keep quarterly reporting. For all investors.

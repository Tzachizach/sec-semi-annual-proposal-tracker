Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Kenneth Fuller

A significant body of academic research demonstrates that security prices adjust quickly to new information and thus the prices in the capital markets are fair (on average) to all investors.  The continuous information flow by companies to investors is extremely important to ensure fair prices across capital markets for all investors - institutional and individual.  If this proposal is enacted, information flow will decrease and full and fair price discovery will be negatively impacted.  This will almost certainly benefit institutional investors at the expense of individual investors.   The mission of the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) is to protect investors; maintain fair, orderly, and efficient markets; and facilitate capital formation.  Enacting a proposal which decreases information flow to the capital markets seems to be in direct contrast to the mission of the Commission.

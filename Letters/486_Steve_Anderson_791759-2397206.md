Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Steve Anderson

As a long-term investor, this idea for reform misses the mark.  I often review specific quarter to quarter changes in business operations to guide long-term investment decisions.  Rolling back modern quarterly reports to some perceived older ideal reduces the timeliness of available information and materially impairs the quality of available information.  If, after several decades of quarterly reports, we find pieces of quarterly reports to be burdensome and repetitive (a premise which is hard to dispute), the Commission serves my interests as an investor by pursuing initiatives to make quarterly reporting both less burdensome to the business and more useful to the investor, not by eliminating them.  In my opinion, the Commission should leverage modern abilities to process information to seek ways to make more and better information available more quickly and more streamlined for investors, rather than reverting to older eras in which less information was available.  After receiving public comments, the Commission should not go backwards to a semiannual reporting structure.

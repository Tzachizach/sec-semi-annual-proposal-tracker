Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Lauren Gaunt

I am a private, individual investor and, if this proposal is enacted, half of the required, reviewed and certified financial filings I get each year would disappear.  This is unacceptable!  I understand a 10-Q filing is reviewed by independent auditors, and the CEO and CFO sign it personally and can be held legally accountable for its contents.  However, any voluntary corporate communications that would replace them carry none of that.

Further, I understand the SEC's own data show that individual investors own the majority of shares in smaller public companies.  And these smaller companies seem to me most likely to take the new option herein proposed.  I would lose the most information about exactly most of the companies I own.   This is unacceptable!   

Lastly, I understand when the SEC previously reviewed this in 2018-2019 the empirical research record all opposed it and the SEC declined to act.  Please decline to act again on this proposal.  Thank you.

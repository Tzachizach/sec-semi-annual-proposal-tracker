This rule change is fine but requires an additional change similar to what Taiwan requires of all of it's publicly listed companies.

"Listed companies in Taiwan must file monthly operating revenue reports by the 10th day of each calendar month for the preceding month. These reports must include operating turnover and self-assessed income." This information is posted without any commentary. Strictly facts of the business.

The market doesn't need to know the quarterly statements and cash flows but it needs to know whether the companies are collecting revenues as they projected during their last semi-annual guidance.

This is not an onerous requirement in the 21st century.

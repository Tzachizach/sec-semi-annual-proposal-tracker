Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Stephen Teixeira

As an individual investor who has benefitted from the SEC's Regulation FD and its requirements, the proposed changes to company disclosure to allow semi-annual reporting by companies represents a complete 180-degree turn toward obfuscation. One of the beauties of the American markets is the ability for an individual to follow their own vision for their portfolio, building for themselves a tool that addresses their needs and goals. Indispensable to that vision is the 10-Q. In an era of access-at-a-cost, the one tool that levels the playing field for the individual investor is the quarterly report, ensuring that all who invest have equal access to the material information. In short, the 10-Q is the most important tool for democratizing access to the information that moves markets and impacts the individual investor. Data from the UK's moves to less-frequent reporting show that reduced reporting did not drive corporate investment or long-term focus; in fact data shows that the factors impacting a company's focus on short-term vs. long-term to reside completely within the company - most typically executive compensation - rather than external factors like reporting requirements and schedules.

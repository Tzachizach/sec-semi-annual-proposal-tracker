Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Jeremiah Schuur

I strongly oppose the proposed SEC rule allowing semiannual rather than quarterly reporting by public companies. The strength and global dominance of U.S. capital markets rests in large part on the availability of timely, reliable, standardized public information. Frequent disclosure reduces information asymmetry, improves price discovery, lowers the cost of capital, and promotes confidence in the fairness and transparency of our markets.

Quarterly reporting is particularly important for individual investors, who do not have access to the private channels, institutional research, or insider relationships available to large financial firms. Reducing the frequency of mandatory reporting would disproportionately benefit sophisticated market participants while increasing informational disadvantage and uncertainty for ordinary Americans whose retirement savings are invested through 401(k)s, pensions, and mutual funds.

At a time when confidence in markets and institutions is critically important, the SEC should strengthen - not weaken - the flow of accurate public information. The transparency and accountability created by regular reporting requirements are a major reason global investors continue to trust and invest in U.S. markets. Moving to semiannual reporting would represent a step backward for market integrity, investor protection, and long-term public confidence.

Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: James Williams

To The Securities and Exchange Commission,

     I am an investor and active stock trader of about eight years, now. Amending S7-2026-15 to allow companies to have the option of semi-annual reporting over the current quarterly requirements would lead to retail traders and investors missing vital, shorter term information that helps to guide them and helps them to make their decisions of investing into companies with more sound confidence. This will inevitably lead to more market-wide volatility where corporate insiders are more likely to have their capital gains while the general public and retail investors are left out with less timely information. The public as a whole deserves to know the most up-to-date financial information possible of any publicly traded company, and by passing this amendment, those relied-upon reports won't be available as quickly, and the above mentioned consequences are sure to ensue. I urge the Commission to reconsider this amendments intended and all possible unintended results.

     -James Williams

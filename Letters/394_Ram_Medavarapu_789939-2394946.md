Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Ram Medavarapu

It is not a good decision to let companies report every 6 months. A lot of things can happen in a 6-month period and small investors don't get to hear from company management thereby missing out on valuable information. You could argue that less reporting requirements will make companies think long term but I am highly skeptical of that proposition. Quarterly reporting helps companies get instant feedback via market price signals and not engage in dubious activities.

It is understandable from a company's perspective where management would like less reporting regulations but individual investors can be the losers because of the gap in communication. Also, companies could loose too because they might not be able to attract knowledgeable and long-term investors.

All said, leave the reporting requirements as they are and lessen the burdens on the amount of documentation they have to report. For example, annual reports have gone from 80 pages to 170 pages or longer in the recent 10 years.

Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Ozan Tuncer

Allowing corporate financial reporting under a semiannual schedule instead of a quarterly schedule would significantly reduce market transparency. Similar shifts in Europe showed that cutting quarterly reports does not actually improve long-term corporate investment or save meaningful costs. Instead, it simply creates a six-month information gap. Institutional investors and hedge funds have the resources to buy alternative data and estimate earnings nearly in real time. On the other hand, retail investors rely entirely on quarterly reports for their investments. Eliminating mandatory quarterly reports would increase the Wall Street's unfair informational advantage over retail investors.

This is at best an absurd mistake. More likely, it is the result of poisonous corruption of the current administration. The direction the SEC should be moving towards is requiring real-time daily reporting of public companies, so the public can see the ebb and flow of business. This could be followed by a quarterly, binding, attestation of the validity of the postings. Instead, This proposal to allow semi-annual reporting would radically disadvantage the majority of American investors. Further the talking point that quarterly reporting carries a heavy cost is a LIE. Tax filings continue to be done quarterly, and so the public reporting should, at a minimum, match such filings.

Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Vernon Ramsey

The elimination of Semi-Annual A 10-Q Reporting is a blatant ploy to put individual investors at a disadvantage.  Use of quarterly provided data is critical for timely of investment trends, (positive/negative); limits timely generation and review of TTM. In addition, these documents, signed by the CEOs and CFOs eliminates plausible deniability of financial position.  
Quarterly A 10-Q Reports highlights trends, (the Canary-in-the-Coal Mine, that indicate changes in business performance; financial strength; company management direction/concerns/changes; risk factors the business face or has solved; litigation (ongoing or anticipated).  All of these factors decide whether additional investment is made; taken under advisement; or shares are sold.  With potentially trillions of dollars held in:
Stock Funds (EFT/ETF); 401(k); 457(b); Thrift Savings; Roth Accounts; 529 Funds; Individual Portfolios, much of this managed by Fund Managers (whom we are left to trust)
Clear, concise information is essential for reasonable investor and investing safety.
Families, individuals, Trusts are relying on these investments for potential future income, retirement; education costs;  charitable gifting; living costs; principle home purchase; legacy sources.  Removal of reporting requirements that help these entities assess risk, diversify assets, and build financial potential in their households and communities does not and will not engender confidence or trust in our Nation's financial  strength, stability and corporate information.  
Prior efforts, in 2018-2019, where the Commission declined to follow through on the submission and a United Kingdom  2014 study, (where 90% of companies continued Reporting by choice), are examples of the confidence and informational valueinvestors place in these mandatory reporting forms.

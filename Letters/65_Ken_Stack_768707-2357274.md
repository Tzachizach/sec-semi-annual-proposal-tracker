Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Ken Stack

Why is this all or none and a scaled back compliance burden is not being considered for companies in say q2 and q3 with start and end year more comprehensive reporting? Or another alternative; a reduced burden where it makes sense across the board with the existing cadence intact.

As the proposal itself noted there are significant differences between public companies. If changing the reporting structure why not have qualification criteria to be met and maintained enabling only qualifying companies earning it along with investor trust the option to reduce their reporting burden? This carrot may incentivize better accounting and reporting behaviors as accounting deviations from accepted standards and outright fraud are not unheard of.

Reducing the compliance burden to encourage companies to go public is attractive but to not do this thoughtfully creates more uncertainty and unknown risk for investors with less public information. It is the latter goal; investor well being that should remain the primary focus here. Existing investors need a vote on their holdings before reporting levels at their investment decrease at a bare minimum. To follow one's investment through social media as a primary source of information as mentioned in the proposal is incredibly discouraging.

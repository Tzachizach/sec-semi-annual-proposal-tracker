I’m a retail investor, and I do not support moving public companies away from quarterly reporting. 

Quarterly reports matter because ordinary investors do not have the same access as executives, insiders, lawyers, bankers, or large institutions. If companies can wait six months between reports, regular people are the ones left guessing. 

I understand the argument that companies want less reporting burden, but the cost of less transparency falls on investors like me. My retirement savings, ETFs, and long-term investments depend on timely public information. Six months is too long for a company to go dark. 

This should not be optional either. Once some companies move to semiannual reporting, others will feel pressure to follow. That means less information becomes the new standard. 

Please keep quarterly reporting in place.

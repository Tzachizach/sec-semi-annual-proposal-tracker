I am Shannon Leigh Broughton-Smith, a homemaker, and my husband is a physician, with a retirement date of July of 2028 at age 70 and 2 months, chosen because our youngest child, still in college due to learning disabilities, will turn 26 then and no longer be able to stay on employer provided health care. The quality of our retirement and our legacy to our four kids will very much depend on the performance of our 401k retirement fund, and anything that creates less transparency in that fund both for us and for professional fund management will make it much harder for us to insure that our funds not be depleted during what we expect to be a long retirement, since both of us are health conscious individuals from long-lived families. We are not young workers just starting out, with time to make up for poor market performance. After 2028 what we start with will be what we have. My husband put over four decades of his life, working long hours and doing his best to improve the health and well being of our community, while I worked to provide a supportive and values-based upbringing for our two biological children and our two children adopted from foster care. Please do not jeopardize the years of rewarding retirement that we have spent our lives earning by making our investments less transparent with this new rule. Secrecy builds uncertainty, and lowers accountability, and is not a public good. 

Thank you for your time and consideration, 

Shannon Leigh Broughton-Smith

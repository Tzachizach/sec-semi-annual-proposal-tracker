Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Michael Zawitkowski

I strongly oppose the move to only collect information from companies on a half year basis instead of quarterly. It is hard enough to get information about the companies. In the private market no responsible investor and fiduciary would check in on their investments only twice yearly, in fact there are institutional investors that expect monthly updates. Companies are not required to put themselves on the public market to allow outsiders to speculate, but if they do they should honor the responsibility to provide more information, not less. Furthermore, we are living in a world where markets and trends are fluctuating more quickly, not slowing down.

Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Marion Michel

Please do NOT change the reporting requirements for public companies from quarterly to semi-annually.  I rely on information from companies in deciding how to update my portfolio.  In addition, many sources of unbiased investor information rely on company reports, such as Value Line and Morningstar and increasingly, AI.  Investors need factual information on a timely basis to make informed decisions.  By now, all public companies are used to this, and I doubt they find it onerous.  If they do, they probably aren't paying enough attention to running their business.  When important metrics decline, we need to know as soon as possible and vice versa.  Any action that reduces the "public" part of publicly traded companies reduces public trust, in them and in the SEC.

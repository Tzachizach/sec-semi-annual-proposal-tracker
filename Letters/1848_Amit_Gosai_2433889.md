In light of the current state of affairs in the United States, it is concerning to observe the proposal to reduce the frequency of quarterly market reports. This move raises significant concerns about transparency and accountability within the market.

Quarterly reports hold companies accountable to investors on a regular basis, providing timely information that is crucial for informed decision-making. In contrast, a semi-annual report would result in delayed information, potentially leading to a lack of transparency and increased opacity in reporting.

Furthermore, reducing the frequency of quarterly filings may not necessarily lead to cost savings. Critics of quarterly filings often advocate for streamlining them rather than eliminating them, as regular reporting continues to serve investors and maintain market discipline.

It is imperative that the government agency responsible for market regulation prioritize transparency and accountability. By doing so, it can protect small businesses and investors from potential harm and ensure a fair and efficient market.

Pls do your job rather than serving special interests.

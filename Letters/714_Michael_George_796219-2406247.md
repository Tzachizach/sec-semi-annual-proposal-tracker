Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Michael George

To the Securities and Exchange Commission:

I am writing to express my strong opposition to the proposal allowing public companies to transition from quarterly to semiannual financial reporting. Reducing mandatory disclosures to a six-month cadence severely harms retail investors by exacerbating information asymmetry and increasing market volatility. While institutional firms possess the resources to procure alternative data and conduct private channel checks, everyday investors rely entirely on the standardized, verified data found in Form 10-Q filings. Without these 90-day updates, public market data will become rapidly stale, leaving retail investors blind to operational shifts and vulnerable to severe, unpriced stock shocks when semiannual reports are finally released.

Furthermore, stretching the reporting window to 180 days creates a dangerous information vacuum that exponentially increases the risk of insider trading. History, including the high-profile controversies during the Trump administration where individuals used private pandemic briefings to restructure portfolios ahead of the public, demonstrates that individuals with asymmetric access to non-public data will exploit it. Moving to a semiannual schedule lengthens the timeline during which corporate insiders hold material, non-public information, undermining decades of progress in market transparency. I urge the Commission to reject this proposal and maintain the quarterly reporting framework that ensures a fair and level playing field for all market participants.

Quarterly reporting isn’t bureaucracy, it’s transparency. Reducing disclosures to every six months gives insiders and large institutions more time operating with information advantages while retail investors are left in the dark. Less frequent reporting means less accountability, less transparency, and greater uncertainty in the market. If the goal is fair and efficient markets, investors need more timely information, not less.

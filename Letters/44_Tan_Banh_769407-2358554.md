To the Securities and Exchange Commission,
I am writing to express my strong opposition to the proposal to move from quarterly (Form 10-Q) to semiannual (Form 10-S) reporting. While the stated goal is to reduce "short-termism," the actual result will be a dangerous reduction in market transparency that disproportionately harms retail investors. The current quarterly structure is the "gold standard" for a reason. Extending the reporting gap to six months creates a massive "dark period" that gives an unfair advantage to institutional firms and insiders. Large hedge funds and advanced firms already use alternative data such as satellite tracking of retail parking lots, private shipping manifests, and high-frequency credit card data to predict company performance in real time. Retail investors do not have access to these expensive tools; we rely on the 10-Q to level the playing field. By removing the quarterly requirement, the SEC is essentially ensuring that by the time a retail investor sees a semiannual report, the "news" is already six months old and has been fully traded upon by those with better technology. Furthermore, a 180 day gap between financial disclosures creates a breeding ground for insider trading. When material changes in a company's health are not disclosed for half a year, the temptation and opportunity for those with "inside" knowledge to front run the public grow exponentially. I urge the Commission to maintain the current quarterly reporting frequency to ensure the markets remain fair, transparent, and accessible to everyone, not just those with the most advanced tracking technology.

Sincerely,
Tan Banh

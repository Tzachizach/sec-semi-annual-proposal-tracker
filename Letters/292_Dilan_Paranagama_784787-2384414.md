Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Dilan Paranagama

I strongly oppose any proposal to reduce public company reporting from quarterly to semiannual reporting.

Quarterly reporting provides individual and small investors with timely, consistent access to material financial information. Moving to a six-month reporting cycle would create a significant information gap. Large institutional investors, board members, insiders, and others with closer access to company management may still have greater visibility into company performance, while ordinary investors would be left waiting months for basic financial updates.

This would weaken market transparency, reduce investor protection, and make it harder for small investors to make informed decisions. Public markets depend on confidence that all investors have fair and timely access to important information. Less frequent reporting could increase information asymmetry, volatility, and the risk that individual investors are disadvantaged.

While reducing compliance burdens may be a valid goal, it should not come at the expense of transparency and investor protection. The SECâs mission includes protecting investors and maintaining fair, orderly, and efficient markets. Quarterly reporting supports that mission.

I urge the SEC to preserve quarterly reporting requirements for public companies.

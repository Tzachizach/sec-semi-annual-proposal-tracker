The new SEC proposal allowing companies go dark for six months at a time is a terrible idea. While the executives, lawyers, and big institutional investors still know exactly how the business
 is doing, stockholders would be the last to find out. How easy is fraud under these circumstances?  How much do you want to invest in a company that can hide for months? Savings from skipping a public filing are modest legal and accounting fees; furthermore, those savings flow to the company and its executives. The cost of worse information falls the person whose 401(k), IRA, or college fund holds the stock. That's a bad trade for ordinary investors. 
This is just another attempt to scam the working class American.

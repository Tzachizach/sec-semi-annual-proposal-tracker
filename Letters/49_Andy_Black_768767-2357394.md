# Letter 49 — Andy Black

- **Date:** 2026-05-05
- **Role/Affiliation:** Investor
- **Stance:** Oppose
- **Source:** https://www.sec.gov/comments/S7-2026-15/s7202615-768767-2357394.html

---

I respectfully oppose any efforts to loosen or eliminate quarterly reporting requirements for public companies. As an investor, I rely on timely, standardized disclosures to make informed decisions, assess risk, and monitor company performance. Quarterly reporting promotes transparency, market efficiency, and accountability by ensuring that material information is disclosed on a consistent and predictable basis.
Reducing the frequency or rigor of these reports would likely increase information asymmetry, disadvantage retail investors, and diminish confidence in public markets. While concerns about short-termism are valid, they can be addressed through improved disclosure practices and guidance rather than by reducing transparency.
Maintaining robust quarterly reporting standards is essential to preserving investor trust and the integrity of U.S. capital markets.

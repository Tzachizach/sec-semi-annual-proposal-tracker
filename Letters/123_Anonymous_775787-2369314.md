Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Self

The idea of moving to a six-month SEC filing timeline is absolutely ridiculous, and frankly, anyone pushing this nonsense needs a serious reality check. Doubling the time between disclosures is a gift-wrapped invitation for corporate malfeasance, fraud, and the kind of accounting shell games that wiped out shareholders at Enron and WorldCom. Have we learned NOTHING from history?! Investors deserve timely, accurate information about the companies they own, not a black box of silence for half a year while executives quietly dump shares, cook the books, or bury bad news under mountains of stale data. Six months is an eternity in modern markets — entire industries can rise and collapse in that window, and retail investors will be left holding the bag while insiders feast on information asymmetry. The current three-month quarterly cadence isn't some bureaucratic burden, it's the BARE MINIMUM of accountability, and if anything we should be SHORTENING it, not stretching it out to appease lazy executives who can't be bothered to keep honest books. Transparency is the price of accessing public capital — if a company can't handle quarterly reporting, they have absolutely no business being publicly traded in the first place. This proposal isn't reform, it's regression, and it needs to be killed before it does real, lasting damage to market integrity and the millions of ordinary people whose retirements depend on honest, frequent disclosure!

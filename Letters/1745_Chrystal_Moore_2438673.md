My name is Chrystal Moore. I care for my elderly mother and my disabled brother. 
I am a retail investor building financial stability for my family's future. 
Every financial decision I make directly affects my ability to care for the people depending on me. 

That is why I am writing today. 
Quarterly reporting is not a preference. It is a protection. 

I do not have analysts. I do not have insider access. I have my hard-earned money, my responsibilities, and the right to timely information. 

If this proposal passes and companies can go dark for six months, here is what happens to me: 

By the time I get updated information, it may be too late to protect my investments or change my plan. That is not a fair market. That is a trap for people like me. 

This proposal harms retail investors in four clear ways: 

I rely on quarterly updates to make decisions about my retirement savings. Without them, I cannot protect what I have worked for. 

Insiders and institutions will still know what is happening. I will not. That gap is already unfair. This makes it worse. 

Making reduced reporting "optional" does not fix anything. The companies most likely to opt out are exactly the companies whose investors need updates the most. 

Corporate cost savings do not outweigh the harm to people investing slowly, carefully, and without institutional backing. 

I am not a Wall Street investor. I am a caregiver and a retail investor doing my best with what I have. I deserve the same timely access to information that keeps markets honest. 

I urge the SEC to maintain quarterly reporting requirements and uphold its legal duty to protect the investing public — including individuals like me. 

Respectfully submitted, 
Chrystal Moore 
Caregiver | Retail Investor | Community Advocate

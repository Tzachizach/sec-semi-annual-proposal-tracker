Hello to the members of the SEC, 

My name is Iris Morrison. I am a retired communications technician. Part of my retirement income is invested in the stock market, so the idea that companies will be able to conceal their books for six months is frightening. 

How can this even be considered? It's an open invitation to fraud. Business need to be held accountable or they will not be trusted. If they aren't trusted, their stock will not sell. Who wins? A few dishonest business insiders, for a short while. Then smart money will look for countries with better regulations. 

In short, if the SEC can't be depended to have the investors best interests at heart, investors will go elsewhere. 

Thank you for your time.

Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Nancy Boilard

Affiliation: Commercial Insurance WC Underwriter and Retrospective Analyst, Retired

Jun. 10, 2026

 
To whom it may concern,

Please continue to require all companies to report their financial statements on a quarterly basis. Your commission was created to provide oversight for corporations, some of whom may otherwise not have employees’ and investors’ interests in mind and would have no trouble flaunting laws. Your commission is toeing the line to hold all companies accountable to the law.

As a retiree, I rely on investments for my income. The social security safety net to which I contributed for 53 years may not exist in the near future. Keeping corporations accountable by reporting quarterly and providing your oversight plays an important role in ensuring the country’s and any global companies which operate in the US abide by the laws.

Lastly, I used SEC reports extensively in my work while preparing regulatory filings for insurance purposes. I appreciated having necessary merger and acquisitions information available through the filings I retrieved from your website. I needed this information to prepare and submit necessary regulatory filings for insurance filings. Even information such as FEINs, percentage of holdings, and dates of acquisitions were instrumental for me. Oftentimes that information was lacking from application submissions we received to produce the necessary Workers Compensation regulatory filings for compliance.

Please don’t minimize and dilute your role in keeping companies accountable. Please do not allow companies to only report on a semiannual basis. Please keep the quarterly reporting requirements.

I appreciate your time.
Nancy Boilard
Commercial Insurance WC Underwriter and Retrospective Analyst, Retired

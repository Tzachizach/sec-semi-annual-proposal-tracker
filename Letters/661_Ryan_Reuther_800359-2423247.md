Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Ryan Reuther

I strongly oppose the proposed rule permitting optional semiannual reporting on Form 10-S. 
While intended to lower compliance costs, removing the quarterly mandate actively harms market integrity via three distinct mechanisms:

1. Severe Reduction in Liquidity: Empirical data from the 2014 UK and EU regulatory shifts proves that dropping quarterly reports directly causes market liquidity to dry up and bid-ask spreads to permanently widen, increasing transactional friction for all participants.

2. Systemic Adverse Selection: Stable issuers will maintain quarterly disclosures to satisfy credit markets. Form 10-S will primarily be utilized by distressed companies seeking to delay the public disclosure of material risks, creating an opaque sub-market.

3. Sanctioned Information Asymmetry: A 180-day reporting void allows institutional capital to utilize private, high-cost alternative data to track corporate health, while retail investors are left completely blind.  

Please reject Form 10-S to ensure markets remain fair, orderly, and efficient. The savings are not worth the costs.

Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Edward Delana

Expressing strident opposition to this change. 
I rely on these specific quarterly reports in decision making for significant retirement accounts and strategies. 
Reduced transparency in accounting and reporting is unfavorable to average investors. Reduced accounting, filing, and oversight costs will accrue to the companies and management who adopt the new standards, rather than to the investors and owners. Opportunities to hide the ball on actual facts for periods lasting  twice as long  will permit unethical organizations to harm investors and the economy in both quantity and quality.

Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Corporate Governance Class

We have studied governance principles this semester and submit the following considerations for the Commission: 
1. Capital formation efficiency is based on trust.  We believe that mandatory reporting engenders trust due to (a) assurance that `board oversight activities are being conducted regularly, (b) verification processes inspire confidence in the interim reports, including completeness required by a Form 10-Q, (c) the negative assurance that there is no material change is valuable, else the vacuum of information leads to uncertainty.  We believe these factors among others would lead to lower trust, therefore less capital formation efficiency.  
2. The necessity of boards to conduct meaningful oversight and satisfy Caremark duties requires frequent board and management engagement and enterprise risk assessment. Internal and external auditors tend to be engaged regularly in testing. The cost savings may not be as great as hypothesized, and not worth the system cost of lower trust.

Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Justin Smith

I am writing in support of semi-annual reporting requirements. Over the past century, transparency has been fundamental to helping investors make prudent capital decisions. However, the unintended consequences of quarterly reporting have forced corporations to focus narrowly on meeting short-term earnings estimates and satisfying Wall Street analysts, rather than prioritizing core business competencies, long-range planning, and sound capital allocation. Over the past decade, publicly traded companies have increasingly become captive to the quarterly cycle, prioritizing short-term maneuvers—such as reactionary layoffs—to artificially inflate earnings and stock prices at the expense of sustainable growth.

The opportunity to reduce the pressure of reporting to Wall Street every 90 days may help organizations to enhance their focus on running the business rather than simply keeping analysts happy.

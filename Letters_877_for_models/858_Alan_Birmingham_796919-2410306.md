# Letter 858 — Alan Birmingham

Subject: File No. S7-2026-15
From: Alan Birmingham

My name is Alan Birmingham and I am a restaurant worker in Newark, Ohio. It seems pretty fishy to me that the Securities and Exchange Commission should want to reveal workings of the stock exchange less. Particularly at a time when corporate tax breaks are the order of the day, smaller companies fall by the wayside, and disparity between the rich and the poor is at its most stark, you would think that anybody interested in what the market is doing should be better informed. It does not seem like that quarterly reporting should cost so much that it should be unable to be done. It seems, instead, as if this is like is some kind of professional subterfuge. 
  I think that reporting on the market should be done quarterly, as a courtesy to the market to which we all contribute, as opposed to twice a year, which does not seem to me an improvement.

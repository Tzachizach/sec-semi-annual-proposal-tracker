Here are 4 reasons this is a very bad idea:
1) "Optional" is a joke. Once one company does it, competitors will because it is a competitive advantage to hide bad results
2) The savings are miniscule, and will be so diluted on a per chare basis as to be meaningless vs the value of increased reporting and oversight.
3) Business results can change dramatically in 3 months now. 6 Months is way to long for small investors to not know what is happening, while insiders and large investors, M like fund management, do.
4) More time for fraud to grow. How much worse would Enron, Tyco, or Worldcom could have been if data only come out half as often and slowed the raising of questions.

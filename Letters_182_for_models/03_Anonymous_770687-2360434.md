Subject: File No. S7-2026-15

Quarterly reporting drives companies to focus on earnings releases and in turn CEO job security (short term results) instead of focusing on long term results. Investors expect companies to meet or exceed quarterly earnings. This encourages bad behaviors. I witnessed it first-hand working for a large bank involved in the 2016 scandal. While transparency is important for everyone, we need corporate America to start focusing on the big picture and stop chasing quarterly earnings. I'm tired of seeing employees being laid off to help drive earnings. I'm tired of companies taking short cuts because they need to increase their profits to make shareholders happy. We need to get back to making and providing superior products and services. Products that last. Products that don't break in a year because the company chose cheap materials and better quarterly earnings. Afterall, those cheap broken products end up in our landfills.
